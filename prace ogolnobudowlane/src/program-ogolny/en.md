# General course programme

## 1. Course title and short description

**Course title:** Vocational preparation for general construction work (with a specialisation: laying paving blocks).

**Organiser:** EGIDA Legal Aid Foundation (PL: Fundacja pomocy prawnej EGIDA).

The course is a free, one-month vocational training programme intended for foreign nationals legally residing in Poland. It draws on the potential of the partnership project between the SMART Foundation and the EGIDA Foundation, co-financed from the European Social Fund Plus (ESF+).

The most important feature of the course is that it takes place at a **real, active construction site** (PL: budowa). Participants do not learn in a lecture room cut off from reality; instead, they observe every day how the materials and tasks being discussed look in practice, and then practise themselves under the supervision of an experienced practitioner-trainer. The programme places particular emphasis on two pillars: **occupational health and safety (PL: BHP)** and **practical paving skills**.

The teaching materials are prepared in three languages (Polish, English and Spanish), which supports learning for participants with different levels of Polish. At the same time, the course systematically introduces key Polish construction vocabulary, because knowing the basic terms is essential for safe and effective work at a Polish construction site.

After completing the course, a graduate has a genuine chance of being employed at the same construction site where the training took place, as a helper or a paver.

### Introductory mini-glossary

| Polish term | Short explanation |
|---|---|
| budowa | the place where a structure is built or renovated; the work site |
| brukarz | a worker who lays paving blocks and surfaces (a paver) |
| pomocnik | a worker supporting the crew, e.g. with moving materials (a helper) |
| brygadzista (majster) | the person directing the work of a team (a crew) at a workstation (a foreman) |
| BHP | occupational health and safety; rules that protect health and life |
| SOI | personal protective equipment (e.g. helmet, gloves, protective footwear) |

## 2. Target group and entry requirements

### Target group

The course is aimed at foreign nationals who have come to Poland and want to start working in the construction industry but do not have the required construction experience. The group is varied in terms of Polish language skills: from people who know only basic words to those who communicate fluently. For this reason, the course combines learning the trade with the systematic introduction of construction vocabulary and translation support.

### Entry requirements

The requirements are deliberately low so that the course is accessible to the widest possible group of participants:

- **Legal residence in Poland** - confirmed by an appropriate document (e.g. a visa, a residence card, another residence title). This is a formal condition for taking part in the project.
- **Being of age** - having turned 18.
- **General state of health** allowing physical work to be performed (construction work requires physical fitness, lifting and working outdoors). Before being admitted to practical exercises, a declaration of no health contraindications may be required.
- **No requirement of prior experience** in construction or of relevant education.
- **Basic readiness to communicate** - a willingness to learn the basic Polish words needed at the construction site (safety terms and material names). Full language proficiency is not required.
- **Motivation and availability** - readiness to take part in classes over 4 weeks (20 working days), maintaining the required attendance.

## 3. Main objective and detailed objectives

### Main objective

The main objective of the course is to prepare the participant to take up work as a helper or paver at a construction site, with particular preparation for laying paving blocks, built on a strong foundation of occupational health and safety. The graduate is to be ready to perform basic general construction work safely and responsibly, and to independently (under the supervision of a foreman) complete a section of paving-block surface.

### Detailed objectives

The course pursues the following detailed objectives:

1. Equipping the participant with knowledge about the organisation of a construction site, the roles of the participants in the construction process, and the rules of discipline and order on the construction site.
2. Building lasting habits of safe work: correctly using personal protective equipment, recognising hazards and safety signs, and reacting properly in emergency situations.
3. Familiarising the participant with the basic construction materials (aggregates, cement, concrete, mortars, paving blocks, edging, kerbs) and the rules for their storage and transport.
4. Teaching the operation and safe use of basic hand tools, measuring tools and selected equipment (plate compactor (PL: zageszczarka), cut-off saw (PL: przecinarka)).
5. Conveying the skills needed to carry out earthworks and the base course: excavating the formation (PL: korytowanie), profiling and compacting layers, and preparing the bedding layer (PL: podsypka).
6. Training in setting edging and kerbs to line and level along a string line.
7. Practical mastery of laying paving blocks: patterns, joints, laying direction, cutting and fitting, working around manholes, and jointing and compacting the surface.
8. Building the skill of checking the quality of the surface (evenness, falls, joints) and recognising typical defects.
9. Preparing the participant to work as part of a crew, to communicate with the foreman, and to follow good working culture at the construction site.
10. Preparing the participant to take up legal employment in Poland through a review of the formalities (work permit or declaration, contract, employer's OHS training, medical examinations).

## 4. Graduate profile - learning outcomes

After completing the course and passing it successfully, the graduate achieves the learning outcomes described below, grouped into three categories: knowledge, skills and social competences.

### 4.1. Knowledge

The graduate:

- **W1.** knows the basic organisation of a construction site and the roles of the participants in the construction process (site manager, foreman, paver, helper, operator);
- **W2.** knows the legal basis of OHS as well as the most common hazards occurring at a construction site and the rules for dealing with them;
- **W3.** knows the types and purpose of personal protective equipment and the meaning of safety signs and danger zones;
- **W4.** knows the basic construction materials (aggregates, cement, concrete, mortars, paving blocks, edging, kerbs, geotextile (PL: geowloknina)) and the rules for their storage;
- **W5.** knows the names, purpose and rules for the safe use of basic construction tools and equipment;
- **W6.** understands the make-up of a surface structure: the base-course layers, the bedding layer, the role of falls (usually 1-2%) and drainage;
- **W7.** knows the rules for laying paving blocks: patterns, joint width (2-5 mm), the direction and line of laying, and the technique of working from the laid surface (PL: z czola);
- **W8.** knows the criteria for assessing surface quality and the typical defects and ways of repairing them;
- **W9.** knows the basic Polish construction vocabulary needed for safe work and communication within the crew;
- **W10.** knows the general rules for the legal employment of a foreign national in Poland (work permit or declaration, contract, initial OHS training, medical examinations).

### 4.2. Skills

The graduate is able to:

- **U1.** correctly select and use personal protective equipment and keep the workstation tidy;
- **U2.** recognise hazards at a construction site, react to an emergency situation and provide basic first aid;
- **U3.** safely carry out manual handling and the unloading of materials while observing the permissible lifting limits;
- **U4.** use hand and measuring tools (spirit level, mason's string line, screed board, measuring tape) and safely operate the plate compactor and the cut-off saw;
- **U5.** read simple surface drawings, set out the outline, place stakes and string lines, and set the falls;
- **U6.** carry out the formation excavation, profile and compact the base-course layers, and prepare and screed the bedding layer;
- **U7.** set edging and kerbs to line and level along a string line, on a concrete bed (PL: lawa betonowa);
- **U8.** lay paving blocks while keeping the pattern, even joints, line and level, working with the from-the-laid-surface technique (PL: z czola);
- **U9.** cut and fit blocks at kerbs, manholes and corners while observing OHS rules (wet cutting, protection of the eyes, hearing and respiratory tract);
- **U10.** carry out the jointing and compaction of the finished surface and assess its quality (evenness, falls, joints);
- **U11.** independently, as part of a team and under supervision, complete a section of surface from the bedding layer through laying and cutting to jointing.

### 4.3. Social competences

The graduate:

- **K1.** follows OHS rules, the site regulations and work discipline, treating safety as a priority;
- **K2.** cooperates within the crew, communicates with the foreman, and reports problems and hazards;
- **K3.** shows responsibility for the quality of their own work and for the equipment and materials entrusted to them;
- **K4.** takes care to save material and to keep order and good working culture at the construction site;
- **K5.** is ready to keep learning, to improve skills and to accept feedback;
- **K6.** understands the importance of punctuality, reliability and professional ethics in the work environment.

## 5. Course structure - 4 modules (weeks)

The course consists of four modules corresponding to consecutive weeks of training. In total it covers 20 working days. The approximate number of hours per module is about 35 hours (5 days of about 7 hours each).

| Module (week) | Module title | Thematic scope | Number of days | Approximate hours |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Module 1 (Week 1) | Introduction, OHS and construction basics | Site organisation and roles; OHS I and II (hazards, personal protective equipment, particularly dangerous work, first aid); construction materials; tools and equipment; basics of measurement. Test 1. | 5 | approx. 35 h |
| Module 2 (Week 2) | Earthworks, base course and ground preparation | Drawing and measurement, falls; earthworks and formation excavation; the base course and its compaction; edging and kerbs; the bedding layer. Test 2. | 5 | approx. 35 h |
| Module 3 (Week 3) | Laying paving blocks (specialisation) | Laying rules and patterns; laying the surface; cutting and fitting; falls, drainage and utilities; jointing and compaction. Test 3. | 5 | approx. 35 h |
| Module 4 (Week 4) | Finishing, quality, supplementary work and employment | Quality control of the surface; other general construction work; work organisation and quality; integrated practice (the assessed task); final examination and the employment pathway. | 5 | approx. 35 h |

## 6. Number of hours

The course lasts **1 month = 4 weeks = 20 working days** (Monday to Friday). Classes are held for 6-8 hours a day; for programme purposes, about 7 hours of classes a day are assumed. The total length of the course is about **140 hours**.

The approximate distribution of time on a typical training day (excluding breaks):

| Part of the day | Form | Approximate time |
|---|---|---|
| Theory block (morning) | lecture, presentation, discussion of materials (welfare facilities / a designated area) | approx. 2.5 h |
| Observation and exercises | observation of real work at the construction site and practical exercise under supervision | approx. 3.5 h |
| Debrief (summary) | summary of the day, conclusions, questions, ongoing assessment | approx. 0.5 h |

The above split is approximate and may be flexibly adjusted to the course of work at the active construction site and to the weather conditions. On days with tests, part of the time is set aside for revision and the partial test.

## 7. Methodology

The course is based on the principle of **learning by doing** in conditions of real work. The methodology combines three closely linked elements, repeated after each portion of the material:

1. **Theory** - a short, concrete theory block delivered in the morning. The trainer explains the topic in plain language, introduces Polish vocabulary, and uses materials in three languages (PL/EN/ES) and photographs. The theory is subordinate to practice: it explains the "why" and the "how" before the participant sees it at the construction site.
2. **Observation at the active construction site** - after the theory, participants move to the construction site and observe how the material or task being discussed looks and is carried out in reality (e.g. a pallet of paving blocks, the plate compactor at work, setting a kerb). Observation links abstract knowledge with a concrete image.
3. **Practical exercise under supervision** - participants carry out the task themselves under the eye of the practitioner-trainer, who corrects mistakes on the spot. The day ends with a short debrief.

Additional methodological assumptions:

- **Gradual increase in difficulty** - from simple tasks (transport, tidying) to complex ones (laying a field of blocks, cutting, jointing and the integrated task on Day 19).
- **A repeatable daily pattern** - thanks to a fixed structure, participants quickly gain a sense of rhythm and safety.
- **Multilingual support** - materials in three languages and translation support; at the same time, the consistent introduction of Polish construction terms with a short explanation.
- **Safety as a priority** - every practical block begins with a reminder of the OHS rules relevant to the given task.
- **Individual pace within the group** - the trainer adjusts the support to the participant's level, taking care that no one is left behind.
- **Ongoing feedback** - mistakes are corrected immediately, during the exercise, which reinforces correct habits.

## 8. Conditions for delivery

So that the course can achieve its objectives, the following conditions are provided:

- **Access to an active construction site** - classes take place at a real, working construction site, which makes it possible to observe real work and to practise in authentic conditions.
- **Welfare facilities** - a welfare container or a designated area for delivering the theory block, with a space for breaks.
- **Equipment and tools** - a complete set of hand tools (rubber mallet, shovel, rake, wheelbarrow, spirit level, mason's string line, screed board, set square), measuring tools (measuring tape, levelling staff, level, water level (PL: waz wodny), laser level) and equipment (plate compactor, tamper (PL: ubijak), cut-off saw, angle grinder).
- **Construction materials** - aggregates, cement, water, paving blocks of various types, edging, kerbs and geotextile, available in quantities allowing exercises to be carried out.
- **Personal protective equipment (PL: SOI) for participants** - helmets, protective footwear, high-visibility vests, gloves, safety glasses, hearing protection and respiratory protection, in particular when cutting blocks.
- **Staff** - a construction practitioner-trainer / paver with professional experience, leading both the theory and the exercises; language and translation support for participants with different levels of Polish.
- **First-aid kit and emergency procedures** - access to a first-aid kit, marked evacuation routes and an assembly point, in line with the rules applicable at the construction site.

## 9. OHS rules on the course with the legal basis

Occupational health and safety is the foundation of the entire course. The OHS rules apply from the first day to the last, and following them is a condition for taking part in the practical exercises.

### General rules on the course

- every participant wears the required personal protective equipment appropriate to the task being performed (helmet, protective footwear, high-visibility vest, and, where needed, gloves, glasses, hearing protection and respiratory protection);
- before every practical block, the trainer reminds participants of the hazards and the rules for safely performing the given work (workstation instruction);
- participants move only within the designated zones and routes, respecting the danger zones and safety signs;
- when cutting blocks, wet cutting is mandatory, as is protection of the eyes, hearing and respiratory tract, because of the silica dust;
- manual handling is carried out while observing the permissible lifting limits and using the correct lifting technique;
- the participant reports every dangerous situation, breakdown or injury; they know the location of the first-aid kit, the evacuation routes and the assembly point.

### Legal basis

The OHS content on the course is based solely on the following legal acts (the obligations are presented in plain language, as a paraphrase, without quoting the full text of the provisions verbatim):

- **The Act of 26 June 1974 - Labour Code** (PL: Kodeks pracy) (consolidated text), Section X "Occupational health and safety", in particular:
  - Article 207 of the Labour Code - the employer is responsible for the state of occupational health and safety and is obliged to protect the health and life of employees;
  - Article 211 of the Labour Code - the basic duty of the employee is to comply with OHS regulations and rules;
  - Article 237(3) of the Labour Code - an employee must not be allowed to start work without the required OHS training;
  - Articles 237(6)-237(9) of the Labour Code - the obligation to provide personal protective equipment and work clothing and footwear.
- **The Regulation of the Minister of Labour and Social Policy of 26 September 1997 on general occupational health and safety regulations** (consolidated text).
- **The Regulation of the Minister of Infrastructure of 6 February 2003 on occupational health and safety during construction work** (Journal of Laws 2003 No. 47 item 401) - the basic act for work at a construction site.
- **The Regulation of the Minister of Economy of 20 September 2001 on occupational health and safety during the operation of machines and other technical devices for earthworks, construction and road works** - it concerns, among other things, plate compactors.
- **The Regulation of the Minister of Economy and Labour of 27 July 2004 on training in the field of occupational health and safety** - general and workstation instruction.
- **The Act of 7 July 1994 - Construction Law** (PL: Prawo budowlane) - among other things, the role of the site manager and the safety and health protection plan (PL: BIOZ), referred to in Article 21a of the Construction Law.

## 10. Assessment system and conditions for passing

Assessment is continuous and covers both theoretical knowledge and practical skills.

### Assessment elements

| Element | When | What it covers |
|---|---|---|
| Test 1 (partial) | after Module 1 (Day 5) | introduction, OHS, materials, tools |
| Test 2 (partial) | after Module 2 (Day 10) | drawing and measurement, earthworks, base course, edging, bedding layer |
| Test 3 (partial) | after Module 3 (Day 15) | laying rules, cutting, utilities, jointing and compaction |
| Observation and exercise sheets | daily | ongoing assessment of observation and practical exercises |
| Practical assessed task | Day 19 | independent completion of a section of surface as a team, according to the assessment sheet |
| Final examination | Day 20 | theory test and a practical task |

### Conditions for completing the course

Completing the course and receiving a certificate is conditional on jointly meeting the following conditions:

- the required **attendance** at classes;
- **passing the partial tests** (Test 1, Test 2, Test 3);
- a **positive practical assessment** resulting from the observation and exercise sheets and from the assessed task on Day 19;
- a **positive result in the final examination** (the theoretical and practical parts) on Day 20.

A graduate who meets all the conditions receives a **certificate of completion of the course**.

## 11. Course documentation

The delivery of the course is documented in a way that makes it possible to confirm the course of the training and the outcomes achieved. The documentation includes:

- the **general course programme** (this document) and the detailed programme describing the individual days;
- the **course log** - a record of classes, attendance and the topics covered;
- participant **attendance lists**;
- **observation and practical-exercise sheets** - ongoing assessment of progress;
- **partial test sheets** (Test 1, Test 2, Test 3) together with the results;
- the **assessment sheet for the assessed task** (Day 19);
- the **final examination report** (Day 20);
- a **register of issued certificates** of completion of the course;
- **OHS documentation** - confirmations of instruction, participants' declarations, materials on the safety rules;
- **teaching materials** in three languages (PL/EN/ES).

## 12. Employment pathway at the construction site after the course

One of the most important strengths of the course is the realistic prospect of employment. Because the training takes place at an active construction site, the graduate has a chance to stay and take up work in the same place.

The employment pathway includes:

1. An **employment interview** - a discussion of the working conditions, the position (helper or paver) and the employer's expectations. This subject is covered on Day 20.
2. **Setting the position and conditions** - based on the course results and the practical assessment, the graduate may receive a job offer matching their skills.
3. **The formalities of legal work for a foreign national in Poland** (a review): a title authorising work (a work permit or a declaration entrusting the performance of work), concluding a contract, the employer's initial OHS training, and the required medical examinations.
4. The **issuance of the certificate of completion of the course**, which confirms the competences acquired and supports the employment process.

The course prepares the participant not only for physical work but also for understanding the formal conditions of legal employment in Poland, which reduces the risk of misunderstandings and increases the chance of stable work.

**Organiser:** EGIDA Legal Aid Foundation (PL: Fundacja pomocy prawnej EGIDA).
