# Detailed course programme

Course: "Vocational preparation for general construction work (with a specialisation: laying paving blocks)"
Organiser: EGIDA Legal Aid Foundation. The course is free of charge for foreigners legally residing in Poland. It was created from the potential of the partnership project of the SMART and EGIDA Foundations, co-financed by the ESF+.

## How to read this programme

This document describes the course day by day. The course lasts 1 month, that is 4 weeks and 20 working days (Monday to Friday). Each day is about 7 hours of classes (plus breaks), about 140 hours in total. The material is divided into 4 modules. Each module is one week.

The course takes place at a real, working construction site (PL: budowa). The schedule of each day is fixed:

1. A theory block in the morning (about 2.5 h) in the welfare facilities or in a designated area.
2. Observation on the construction site (a portion of about 3.5 h), during which trainees watch how the discussed material or stage of work looks in practice.
3. A supervised practical exercise with the trainer (the remaining part of about 3.5 h).
4. A short daily review (about 0.5 h): summary, questions, conclusions for the next day.

Materials are available in three languages (Polish, English, Spanish) to support people with different levels of Polish. In the content we introduce key Polish construction vocabulary with a short explanation, because on a construction site instructions are given in Polish.

Days with a partial test: day 5, day 10, day 15. Day with the final examination: day 20. Day 19 is the practical final assignment.

Abbreviations used in the text:
- BHP (PL: bezpieczenstwo i higiena pracy) - occupational health and safety.
- SOI (PL: srodki ochrony indywidualnej) - personal protective equipment (hard hat, protective footwear, vest, gloves, goggles, hearing protectors, respiratory protection).
- BIOZ (PL: plan bezpieczenstwa i ochrony zdrowia) - health and safety plan (a document on the construction site).

# MODULE 1 (Week 1): Introduction, OHS and construction basics

Module objective: to introduce the trainee to the world of the construction site. The participant learns how a construction site is organised, who works on it and who is responsible for what, what the safety rules are (this is the foundation of the whole course), and what materials, tools and equipment will be with them throughout the month. Without this knowledge, no one is allowed to enter a workstation. The module ends with the first test (day 5).

## Day 1: Introduction to the course and the construction site

Approximate time: about 7 h (theory about 2.5 h, walk-through and observation about 3.5 h, review about 0.5 h, excluding breaks).

Learning objectives of the day:
- Understanding the purpose of the course and the path that leads to employment.
- Learning the organisation of the construction site and the division of roles.
- Learning the basic documents present on the construction site.
- Mastering the first Polish words used daily on the site.

Theory content:
- Presentation of the course: purpose, schedule, rules of participation, method of assessment.
- What a construction site is and what the stages of the construction process are (from ground zero to finishing).
- Participants in the construction process and their roles: site manager (PL: kierownik budowy), foreman (PL: majster, brygadzista), paving worker (PL: brukarz), helper (PL: pomocnik), machine operator, investor, supervision inspector.
- Documents on the construction site: construction log (PL: dziennik budowy), BIOZ plan, instructions, schedule.
- Site rules and discipline: punctuality, sobriety, no entry to zones without authorisation, obeying the foreman's instructions.
- Basic construction vocabulary in Polish (daily instructions and names).

Scope of practical observation on the construction site:
- A walk-through of the whole site with the trainer.
- Identification of zones: storage zone, work zone, access route, welfare facilities, danger zone.
- Recognition of workstations and who works where.

Practical exercise:
- The trainee independently points out the individual zones on the plan and in the field, and names in Polish three participants in the construction process and their tasks.

Learning outcomes of the day:
- The trainee knows the purpose of the course and understands their path to employment.
- They can name the main roles on the construction site and say who gives them instructions.
- They recognise the zones on the construction site and know where it is and is not allowed to go.

Key terms (in Polish):
- Budowa (construction site) - the place where a structure is built; the work area.
- Kierownik budowy (site manager) - the person responsible for the entire course and safety of the construction.
- Majster, brygadzista (foreman) - the person managing the crew, who gives daily instructions.
- Brukarz (paving worker) - a worker specialising in laying paving blocks.
- Pomocnik (helper) - a worker supporting the crew (transport, preparation, tidiness).
- Dziennik budowy (construction log) - the official document recording the course of the works.
- Zaplecze socjalne (welfare facilities) - a container or room for rest, changing, and meals.
- Strefa niebezpieczna (danger zone) - an area of increased risk (e.g. crane operation, excavation).

## Day 2: OHS I - safety basics and protective equipment

Approximate time: about 7 h (theory about 2.5 h, observation and exercise about 3.5 h, review about 0.5 h, excluding breaks).

Learning objectives of the day:
- Understanding why OHS is the most important thing on a construction site.
- Learning the most common hazards and ways to avoid them.
- Mastering the correct selection and use of personal protective equipment (SOI).

Theory content:
- The legal basis of OHS, described in plain language: the Labour Code of 26 June 1974, Section X on occupational health and safety. The employer is obliged to ensure safe working conditions (art. 207 of the Labour Code), and the employee is obliged to comply with OHS rules and regulations (art. 211 of the Labour Code). An employee must not be allowed to work without the required OHS training (art. 237(3) of the Labour Code). The employer provides personal protective equipment and work clothing (art. 237(6) to 237(9) of the Labour Code).
- General OHS rules arising from the Regulation of the Minister of Labour and Social Policy of 26 September 1997 on general health and safety at work provisions.
- The most common hazards on a construction site: falling, tripping, being struck, being crushed, cuts, contact with electricity, noise, dust.
- Personal protective equipment (SOI): hard hat, protective footwear, high-visibility vest, gloves, safety goggles, hearing protectors, respiratory protection. When and why we use each of them.
- Safety signs: prohibition, mandatory, warning, evacuation, fire protection.
- Tidiness at the workstation as an element of safety.

Scope of practical observation on the construction site:
- Observation of how workers use SOI at real workstations.
- Recognition of the safety signs placed around the construction site.
- Pointing out the danger zones and the site information board.

Practical exercise:
- Independent, correct putting on of a full set of SOI and checking their condition (whether the hard hat is not cracked, whether the footwear has a toe cap, whether the goggles are not scratched).
- Matching the appropriate SOI to a given situation (e.g. cutting - goggles and hearing protectors).

Learning outcomes of the day:
- The trainee understands that OHS is a condition for being allowed to work, not a formality.
- They can independently select and correctly put on SOI.
- They recognise the basic safety signs.

Key terms (in Polish):
- BHP (OHS) - occupational health and safety.
- SOI (PPE) - personal protective equipment.
- Kask, helm (hard hat) - head protection, mandatory on the construction site.
- Obuwie ochronne (protective footwear) - boots with a toe cap and a hard sole.
- Kamizelka odblaskowa (high-visibility vest) - makes the worker visible.
- Ochronniki sluchu (hearing protectors) - protect the ears from noise.
- Znak nakazu (mandatory sign) - a round blue sign (e.g. hard hat required).
- Strefa niebezpieczna (danger zone) - a place of increased accident risk.

## Day 3: OHS II - dangerous work, first aid and procedure after an accident

Approximate time: about 7 h (theory about 2.5 h, observation and exercise about 3.5 h, review about 0.5 h, excluding breaks).

Learning objectives of the day:
- Learning particularly dangerous work and the rules of safe work in such conditions.
- Mastering the rules of manual transport and lifting.
- Learning the basics of first aid and the procedure in the event of an accident and evacuation.

Theory content:
- Particularly dangerous work on a construction site and the obligation of on-the-job instruction. Basis: the Regulation of the Minister of Infrastructure of 6 February 2003 on occupational health and safety during the performance of construction works (Journal of Laws 2003 No. 47 item 401).
- The basics of work at height: what work at height is, why fall protection is needed.
- Hazards at excavations: wall collapse, falling in, underground utilities.
- Manual transport work and lifting: safe lifting technique (straight back, bent knees), permissible load limits, working in pairs with heavy elements.
- Electricity and electric tools, noise and dust as health hazards.
- First aid: calling for help, basic actions, locating the first aid kit.
- Procedure in the event of an accident: stopping work, securing the area, reporting to the foreman, emergency numbers (112).
- Evacuation: evacuation routes, assembly point, alarm signal.
- OHS training: general and on-the-job instruction according to the Regulation of the Minister of Economy and Labour of 27 July 2004 on training in the field of occupational health and safety. The role of the BIOZ plan according to the Construction Law of 7 July 1994 (art. 21a).

Scope of practical observation on the construction site:
- A demonstration of on-the-job instruction.
- Locating the first aid kit, fire extinguisher, evacuation routes and assembly point.
- Observation of excavation protections or a work-at-height zone (if present).

Practical exercise:
- Practising the correct technique of lifting a load and carrying it in pairs.
- Simulation of reporting an accident: who notifies whom and how, what information to pass on.

Learning outcomes of the day:
- The trainee knows how to safely lift and carry loads.
- They can describe what to do when they notice an accident.
- They know the evacuation route and assembly point on their construction site.

Key terms (in Polish):
- Prace szczegolnie niebezpieczne (particularly dangerous work) - high-risk work requiring additional measures.
- Praca na wysokosci (work at height) - work above a defined height, with a risk of falling.
- Wykop (excavation) - a recess in the ground, e.g. for a foundation or utilities.
- Reczne prace transportowe (manual transport work) - carrying and moving loads by human force.
- Apteczka (first aid kit) - a set for providing first aid.
- Punkt zborny (assembly point) - the gathering place after an evacuation.
- Instruktaz stanowiskowy (on-the-job instruction) - OHS training for a specific workstation.
- Numer alarmowy 112 (emergency number 112) - one number for all emergency services.

## Day 4: Construction materials

Approximate time: about 7 h (theory about 2.5 h, observation and exercise about 3.5 h, review about 0.5 h, excluding breaks).

Learning objectives of the day:
- Learning the basic materials used for paving block surfaces.
- Understanding what each material is for.
- Mastering the rules of storage, transport and unloading of materials (with an emphasis on OHS).

Theory content:
- Aggregates: sand, crushed stone fines, crushed stone; the concept of a fraction (grain size, e.g. 0-2 mm, 4-31.5 mm) and what each fraction is used for.
- Cement and water: the role of the binder, the basics of mixing.
- Concrete and mortars: how they differ, where they are used.
- Concrete paving blocks: types, thicknesses of 6 cm (pedestrian areas) and 8 cm (vehicle traffic), shapes (cegielka, behaton, fala - "brick", double-T, "wave"), colours.
- Edge restraints and kerbs (PL: obrzeza, krawezniki): the role of bordering the surface and providing lateral resistance.
- Geotextile (PL: geowloknina): a separation layer between the soil and the sub-base.
- Storage and palletising: stable stacking of pallets, markings, binding strap.
- Manual transport and unloading: OHS rules, teamwork, avoiding being crushed.

Scope of practical observation on the construction site:
- A walk-around of the material storage area on the construction site.
- Looking at pallets of paving blocks, their markings (type, thickness, colour, quantity in m2).
- Recognition of different aggregates and their fractions.

Practical exercise:
- Recognition and naming in Polish of several materials (6 cm and 8 cm blocks, kerb, edge restraint, sand, crushed stone fines).
- Safe carrying of several elements from a pallet to the storage place, in pairs.

Learning outcomes of the day:
- The trainee recognises and names the basic materials and knows what they are for.
- They understand the difference between 6 cm and 8 cm blocks.
- They can safely unload and set down material.

Key terms (in Polish):
- Kruszywo (aggregate) - a loose mineral material (sand, gravel, crushed stone).
- Frakcja (fraction) - a range of aggregate grain sizes.
- Kliniec (crushed stone fines) - fine crushed aggregate for the sub-base and bedding.
- Tluczen (crushed stone) - coarse crushed aggregate for load-bearing layers.
- Kostka brukowa (paving block) - a concrete surfacing element.
- Behaton (double-T block) - a popular block shape (double-T type).
- Krawieznik (kerb) - a bordering element for a road or surface.
- Obrzeze (edge restraint) - a low bordering element for a pavement or lawn.
- Geowloknina (geotextile) - a fabric separating layers of soil and sub-base.
- Paleta (pallet) - a wooden base for storing and transporting material.

## Day 5: Tools and equipment. REVIEW + TEST 1

Approximate time: about 7 h (theory and review about 2.5 h, observation and exercise about 3.5 h, test and review about 0.5 h, excluding breaks).

Note: on this day the first partial test (Test 1) takes place, covering the material of module 1.

Learning objectives of the day:
- Learning hand tools, measuring tools and power tools, as well as compaction equipment.
- Mastering the rules of safe tool use.
- Consolidating and checking the knowledge from the entire first week.

Theory content:
- Hand tools: rubber mallet, shovel, rake, wheelbarrow, spirit level, mason's line, screed board, set square. What each of them is for.
- Measuring tools: tape measure, optical level (PL: niwelator) and levelling staff, water level (PL: waz wodny), laser level. Why we measure and how.
- Power tools: cut-off saw, angle grinder. Hazards and protection.
- Compaction equipment: plate compactor (vibrating) (PL: zageszczarka plytowa) and rammer (the so-called jumping jack) (PL: ubijak, skoczek). Basis: the Regulation of the Minister of Economy of 20 September 2001 on occupational health and safety during the operation of machines for earthworks, construction and road works.
- Tool OHS: checking the condition, guards, correct grip, putting away after work.
- The basics of measurement and setting out: line, level, reference point.

Scope of practical observation on the construction site:
- Observation of a plate compactor at work.
- A demonstration of using an optical level or laser level to check the level.

Practical exercise:
- Recognition and naming in Polish of the tools from the set.
- Checking the condition of a spirit level and using it to check the level of a flat surface.
- Test 1: a short written or oral test (with the possibility of language support) on the material of module 1.

Learning outcomes of the day:
- The trainee recognises and names the basic tools and equipment.
- They can safely grip a tool and check its condition.
- They pass Test 1 covering the organisation of the construction site, OHS, materials and tools.

Key terms (in Polish):
- Mlotek gumowy (rubber mallet) - for seating blocks without damaging them.
- Poziomica (spirit level) - an instrument for checking level and plumb.
- Lata (screed board) - a long, straight strip for screeding and checking flatness.
- Sznur murarski (mason's line) - a tensioned line marking a line and level.
- Niwelator (optical level) - an instrument for measuring differences in height (levels).
- Zageszczarka plytowa (plate compactor) - a vibrating machine for compaction.
- Ubijak, skoczek (rammer, jumping jack) - equipment for compaction in narrow places.
- Wytyczanie (setting out) - marking out the position and shape of the surface in the field.

# MODULE 2 (Week 2): Earthworks, sub-base and ground preparation

Module objective: to teach the trainee everything that happens beneath the blocks. A good surface starts with a solid base. The participant learns to read simple drawings, set out and measure falls, perform earthworks, build the sub-base in layers, set edge restraints and kerbs, and prepare the bedding. These stages determine the durability of the paving. The module ends with the second test (day 10).

## Day 6: Drawing and measurement

Approximate time: about 7 h (theory about 2.5 h, observation and exercise about 3.5 h, review about 0.5 h, excluding breaks).

Learning objectives of the day:
- Mastering the reading of a simple surface drawing.
- Understanding falls and the direction of water runoff.
- Learning the rules of setting out the outline and staking.

Theory content:
- Reading simple drawings and a surface design: plan view, dimensions, markings.
- Levels (heights of points) and benchmarks (fixed height reference points).
- Falls: a minimum of 1-2% for proper drainage; how to calculate a fall (e.g. 2% is 2 cm per 1 m).
- Direction of water runoff: water must flow away from the building towards the drainage.
- Setting out the outline: staking, lines, checking right angles.

Scope of practical observation on the construction site:
- Observation of the crew setting out a surface.
- A demonstration of how a line and stakes mark out the edge and level.

Practical exercise:
- Reading the dimensions and direction of fall from a simple drawing.
- Placing the stakes and tensioning the line along the marked-out line.

Learning outcomes of the day:
- The trainee reads basic information from a simple drawing.
- They understand what a fall is and why it is necessary.
- They can help to set out the outline using stakes and a line.

Key terms (in Polish):
- Rysunek, projekt (drawing, design) - a graphic plan of the surface with dimensions.
- Rzedna (level) - the height of a point relative to an adopted datum.
- Reper (benchmark) - a fixed height reference point.
- Spadek (fall) - the slope of a surface, given as a percentage.
- Odwodnienie (drainage) - the removal of water from the surface.
- Obrys (outline) - the line defining the shape and boundary of the surface.
- Palikowanie (staking) - driving in stakes that mark out a line.
- Sznur prowadzacy (guide line) - a tensioned line indicating a line and level.

## Day 7: Earthworks

Approximate time: about 7 h (theory about 2.5 h, observation and exercise about 3.5 h, review about 0.5 h, excluding breaks).

Learning objectives of the day:
- Learning the stages of earthworks for a surface.
- Understanding the depth of the formation (PL: koryto) and its dependence on the structure.
- Mastering the OHS rules for excavations.

Theory content:
- Removal of topsoil (the living layer of earth) and its haulage away.
- Box excavation (PL: korytowanie): excavating soil to a defined depth.
- The depth of the formation depends on the surface structure (the sum of the thicknesses of all the layers plus the blocks).
- Excavation OHS: safe slope of the embankments, securing the walls, caution around underground utilities (cables, pipes). Basis: the Regulation of 6 February 2003 on OHS during construction works.
- Haulage of the spoil (the excavated soil).
- Manual and machine work; cooperation with the operator.
- Compaction of the formation bottom.

Scope of practical observation on the construction site:
- Observation of box excavation and levelling (evening out) of the bottom.
- A demonstration of how the depth and flatness of the bottom are checked.

Practical exercise:
- Manually evening out a section of the formation bottom with a shovel and rake.
- Checking the depth against the reference line.

Learning outcomes of the day:
- The trainee understands what box excavation is and why we do it.
- They know the rules of safe work at an excavation.
- They can help to even out and check the formation bottom.

Key terms (in Polish):
- Humus (topsoil) - the fertile, living layer of earth that is removed.
- Korytowanie (box excavation) - excavating soil for the surface structure.
- Koryto (formation) - the excavated recess for the surface layers.
- Skarpa (embankment) - the sloped wall of an excavation.
- Media podziemne (underground utilities) - cables and pipes in the ground.
- Urobek (spoil) - the soil excavated during earthworks.
- Niwelacja (levelling) - evening out a surface to a required level.
- Zageszczanie (compaction) - tamping the soil so that it is stable and load-bearing.

## Day 8: Sub-base

Approximate time: about 7 h (theory about 2.5 h, observation and exercise about 3.5 h, review about 0.5 h, excluding breaks).

Learning objectives of the day:
- Learning the structural layers of the surface.
- Understanding the principle of layer-by-layer compaction.
- Learning the role of water and geotextile.

Theory content:
- Structural layers: the drainage layer (free-draining) and the main (load-bearing) sub-base.
- Crushed aggregates for the sub-base (crushed stone fines, crushed stone, mixes).
- Layer thicknesses and their profiling (giving shape and fall).
- Placing and compacting in layers: we compact in thin layers, not the whole thickness at once.
- Compaction index: a measure of whether a layer is sufficiently tamped.
- The role of water: the right moisture content makes compaction easier.
- Geotextile and separation: separating the soil from the sub-base so the layers do not mix.

Scope of practical observation on the construction site:
- Observation of laying and compacting successive sub-base layers.
- A demonstration of profiling a layer to the required fall.

Practical exercise:
- Spreading and initial levelling of a layer of aggregate on a designated section.
- Helping with compaction using a plate compactor under supervision (observing OHS).

Learning outcomes of the day:
- The trainee knows the surface layers and their order.
- They understand why we compact in layers and why we add water.
- They can spread and level a layer of aggregate.

Key terms (in Polish):
- Podbudowa (sub-base) - the load-bearing layer beneath the bedding and blocks.
- Warstwa odsaczajaca (drainage layer) - a layer carrying water away from the structure.
- Kruszywo lamane (crushed aggregate) - aggregate from crushed rock.
- Profilowanie (profiling) - giving a layer its shape and fall.
- Zageszczanie warstwami (compaction in layers) - tamping successive thin layers.
- Wskaznik zageszczenia (compaction index) - a measure of how well a layer is tamped.
- Separacja (separation) - separating layers with geotextile.
- Wilgotnosc (moisture content) - the water content that makes compaction easier.

## Day 9: Edge restraints and kerbs

Approximate time: about 7 h (theory about 2.5 h, observation and exercise about 3.5 h, review about 0.5 h, excluding breaks).

Learning objectives of the day:
- Learning the role of bordering the surface.
- Understanding the construction of a concrete bedding with a haunch.
- Mastering the rules of setting a kerb and edge restraint to line and level.

Theory content:
- Why bordering: kerbs and edge restraints hold the blocks in place and prevent them from spreading apart.
- Concrete bedding with a haunch (PL: lawa betonowa z oporem): a concrete base under the element, with an additional haunch at the back for stability.
- Setting the kerb and edge restraint to the line: keeping the line and level.
- Joints between elements: even gaps.
- Curves and corners: fitting elements at bends and corners.
- Concreting the bedding and the haunch.

Scope of practical observation on the construction site:
- Observation of the crew setting kerbs.
- A demonstration of checking line and level using a line and spirit level.

Practical exercise:
- Helping to lay a concrete bedding on a short section.
- Setting an edge restraint to the line and checking the level, with correction using a rubber mallet.

Learning outcomes of the day:
- The trainee understands the role of bordering the surface.
- They know what a concrete bedding with a haunch is.
- They can set an edge restraint to line and level under supervision.

Key terms (in Polish):
- Krawieznik (kerb) - a tall bordering element (e.g. along a road).
- Obrzeze (edge restraint) - a low bordering element (e.g. along a pavement).
- Lawa betonowa (concrete bedding) - the concrete base under a kerb or edge restraint.
- Opor (haunch) - additional concrete behind the element for stability.
- Spoina (joint) - the gap between adjacent elements.
- Luk (curve) - a bend in the bordering.
- Naroznik (corner) - a corner of the bordering.
- Linia (line) - the straight run of an edge marked out with a line.

## Day 10: Bedding. REVIEW + TEST 2

Approximate time: about 7 h (theory and review about 2.5 h, observation and exercise about 3.5 h, test and review about 0.5 h, excluding breaks).

Note: on this day the second partial test (Test 2) takes place, covering the material of module 2.

Learning objectives of the day:
- Learning the types and thickness of bedding.
- Mastering screeding the bedding with a screed board along the guide rails.
- Consolidating and checking the knowledge from the second week.

Theory content:
- Types of bedding: cement-sand (about 1:4, that is 1 part cement to 4 parts sand) and sand.
- Bedding thickness: 3-5 cm after compaction.
- Guide rails (spacer pipes): pipes laid as guides for even screeding.
- Screeding with a screed board: moving the board along the guide rails to obtain an even surface.
- Flatness and fall of the bedding.
- The rule of not walking on the finished bedding, so as not to disturb it.

Scope of practical observation on the construction site:
- Observation of preparing and screeding the bedding.
- A demonstration of using guide rails and a screed board.

Practical exercise:
- Spreading the bedding, laying the guide rails and screeding with the board on a designated area.
- Checking the flatness and fall of the finished bedding.
- Test 2: a short written or oral test on the material of module 2.

Learning outcomes of the day:
- The trainee knows the types and thickness of bedding.
- They can screed the bedding with a board along the guide rails.
- They pass Test 2 covering earthworks, the sub-base, bordering and bedding.

Key terms (in Polish):
- Podsypka (bedding) - the layer on which the blocks are laid directly.
- Podsypka cementowo-piaskowa (cement-sand bedding) - a mix of sand and cement.
- Prowadnica (guide rail) - a pipe marking the level for screeding the bedding.
- Sciaganie lata (screeding with a board) - levelling the bedding by drawing a board across it.
- Rura dystansowa (spacer pipe) - a pipe acting as a guide for the bedding thickness.
- Rownosc (flatness) - the absence of unevenness on the surface.
- Spadek podsypki (bedding fall) - the slope in accordance with the design.
- Gotowa podsypka (finished bedding) - the levelled layer that is not walked on.

# MODULE 3 (Week 3): Laying paving blocks (specialisation)

Module objective: this is the heart of the course and the main specialisation. The participant learns to lay paving blocks: they learn the patterns and rules, lay surfaces, cut and fit blocks around obstacles, work around inspection chambers, and joint and compact the finished surface. After this module, the trainee can, under supervision, independently lay a section of paving. The module ends with the third test (day 15).

## Day 11: Rules of laying blocks

Approximate time: about 7 h (theory about 2.5 h, observation and exercise about 3.5 h, review about 0.5 h, excluding breaks).

Learning objectives of the day:
- Learning the laying patterns and the rules for joints.
- Understanding the rule of laying "from the front" (PL: z czola).
- Mastering laying the first row as the base.

Theory content:
- Laying patterns: brick bond, herringbone 45 degrees and 90 degrees, running bond. Differences and applications.
- Joints of 2-5 mm: why we do not lay blocks "tight" (space is needed for sand in the joint).
- Laying "from the front": laying from the already-laid surface, so as not to walk on the prepared bedding.
- Direction and line of laying, the guide line.
- The first row as the base: the flatness of the whole surface depends on it.

Scope of practical observation on the construction site:
- Observation of an experienced paving worker laying the first rows.
- A demonstration of different patterns on a real surface.

Practical exercise:
- Laying the first rows of blocks to the line, keeping the joints and pattern, using the "from the front" technique.

Learning outcomes of the day:
- The trainee recognises the basic laying patterns.
- They understand why joints are left and why we lay "from the front".
- They can lay the first row as the base.

Key terms (in Polish):
- Wzor ukladania (laying pattern) - the way the blocks are arranged (e.g. herringbone).
- Cegielka (brick bond) - a simple pattern like laying bricks.
- Jodelka (herringbone) - a diagonal pattern (45 or 90 degrees), very load-bearing.
- Spoina (joint) - the gap between blocks (2-5 mm).
- Uklad "z czola" (laying "from the front") - laying from the finished surface, without walking on the bedding.
- Sznur prowadzacy (guide line) - a line marking out the line of the row.
- Pierwszy rzad (first row) - the initial line of blocks, the base for the rest.
- Kierunek ukladania (direction of laying) - the side towards which the work progresses.

## Day 12: Laying a surface

Approximate time: about 7 h (theory about 2.5 h, observation and exercise about 3.5 h, review about 0.5 h, excluding breaks).

Learning objectives of the day:
- Mastering laying a larger area while keeping the pattern and joints.
- Learning to control line, level and to correct with a mallet.
- Understanding the mixing of blocks from several pallets and the role of expansion joints.

Theory content:
- Keeping the pattern and even joints across the whole surface.
- Controlling line and level (line, spirit level, screed board).
- Correcting the position of a block with a rubber mallet.
- Mixing blocks from several pallets at the same time: different pallets may have slightly different shades, mixing prevents colour patches.
- Expansion joints: gaps that allow the surface to move.
- The pace of work and rhythm in the team.

Scope of practical observation on the construction site:
- Observation of laying a larger area.
- A demonstration of mixing blocks from pallets and controlling colour.

Practical exercise:
- Laying an area of blocks while keeping the pattern, even joints and level.
- Correcting individual blocks with a rubber mallet.

Learning outcomes of the day:
- The trainee lays a surface while keeping the pattern and joints.
- They control level and line and correct blocks with a mallet.
- They know why blocks from several pallets are mixed.

Key terms (in Polish):
- Plaszczyzna (surface) - the even surface of the laid blocks.
- Rowne spoiny (even joints) - equal gaps between blocks.
- Korekta (correction) - adjusting the position of a block.
- Mlotek gumowy (rubber mallet) - a tool for seating blocks without damage.
- Mieszanie palet (mixing pallets) - combining blocks from different pallets for an even colour.
- Dylatacja (expansion joint) - a gap compensating for movements of the surface.
- Poziom (level) - conformity with the required height and fall.
- Tempo pracy (pace of work) - an even speed of laying.

## Day 13: Cutting and fitting blocks

Approximate time: about 7 h (theory about 2.5 h, observation and exercise about 3.5 h, review about 0.5 h, excluding breaks).

Learning objectives of the day:
- Learning the tools for cutting blocks.
- Mastering cutting to fit at kerbs, inspection chambers and corners.
- Understanding the OHS rules for cutting (crystalline silica dust).

Theory content:
- Cutting tools: table cut-off saw, angle cut-off saw (grinder), block splitter (guillotine).
- Cutting to fit at kerbs, inspection chambers and corners.
- Marking out and marking the cutting line.
- Cutting OHS: crystalline silica dust is harmful to the lungs, so we cut wet (with water), use eye, hearing and respiratory protection, and a blade guard. Basis: the OHS regulations for construction works of 6 February 2003.

Scope of practical observation on the construction site:
- A demonstration of wet cutting with a table cut-off saw.
- Observation of cutting blocks to fit at an inspection chamber.

Practical exercise:
- Marking the cutting line and making a cut to fit on the cut-off saw under supervision (with full SOI and wet cutting).
- Fitting the cut block at the kerb.

Learning outcomes of the day:
- The trainee knows the cutting tools and their uses.
- They can mark out and safely make a cut to fit.
- They understand the hazard of crystalline silica dust and use protection.

Key terms (in Polish):
- Ciecie (cutting) - trimming a block to size.
- Przecinarka stolowa (table cut-off saw) - a machine for wet cutting of blocks.
- Szlifierka katowa (angle grinder) - a hand-held power tool for cutting.
- Gilotyna (block splitter, guillotine) - a device for splitting blocks without dust.
- Docinka (cut piece) - a trimmed block fitted to a place.
- Trasowanie (marking out) - marking the cutting line on a block.
- Pyl krzemionkowy (crystalline silica dust) - harmful dust created during dry cutting.
- Ciecie na mokro (wet cutting) - cutting with water, which suppresses the dust.

## Day 14: Falls, drainage and utility fittings

Approximate time: about 7 h (theory about 2.5 h, observation and exercise about 3.5 h, review about 0.5 h, excluding breaks).

Learning objectives of the day:
- Mastering working blocks around utility fittings.
- Understanding the height adjustment of inspection chambers and gullies.
- Reinforcing the maintenance of the fall around the elements.

Theory content:
- Utility fittings in the surface: inspection chambers, gullies (drainage gratings), manhole covers, channel drains (linear drainage).
- Fitting blocks to inspection chambers and manhole covers: cuts around round and square elements.
- Height adjustment of the elements: the chamber must be at the level of the surface, slightly below, so that it does not protrude.
- Maintaining the fall around the elements, so that water flows to the gullies.
- Working around the elements: appearance and tightness.

Scope of practical observation on the construction site:
- Observation of adjusting a chamber to the level of the surface.
- A demonstration of working blocks around a gully or a channel drain.

Practical exercise:
- Cutting and laying blocks around an inspection chamber while maintaining the fall.
- Checking that water flows in the correct direction (a test with water or a spirit level).

Learning outcomes of the day:
- The trainee recognises the utility fittings in the surface.
- They can work blocks around an inspection chamber while maintaining the fall.
- They understand why a chamber should be slightly below the surface.

Key terms (in Polish):
- Uzbrojenie (utility fittings) - installation elements in the surface (chambers, gullies).
- Studzienka (inspection chamber) - an access element to an underground installation.
- Wpust (gully) - a grating carrying water away to the sewer.
- Wlaz (manhole cover) - the cover of an inspection chamber.
- Korytko liniowe (channel drain) - drainage in the form of a long channel.
- Regulacja wysokosci (height adjustment) - setting an element at the level of the surface.
- Obrobka (working around) - fitting blocks around an element.
- Spadek do wpustu (fall to the gully) - the slope directing water to the grating.

## Day 15: Jointing and compaction. REVIEW + TEST 3

Approximate time: about 7 h (theory and review about 2.5 h, observation and exercise about 3.5 h, test and review about 0.5 h, excluding breaks).

Note: on this day the third partial test (Test 3) takes place, covering the material of module 3.

Learning objectives of the day:
- Mastering jointing (filling the joints).
- Understanding compacting blocks with a guard.
- Learning the correct sequence of finishing work.

Theory content:
- Filling the joints: sweeping sand or quartz sand into the joints; optionally stabilised (hardened) joints.
- Sweeping: spreading sand over the surface with a broom so that it enters the joints.
- Compacting the surface with a plate compactor fitted with a guard or rubber mat: we never compact blocks directly (without a guard), because of cracking and scratching.
- The sequence of work: jointing, then compaction, then re-jointing (topping up the joints that have settled).
- Aftercare and cleanliness of the finished surface.

Scope of practical observation on the construction site:
- Observation of jointing and compacting an area.
- A demonstration of using a rubber mat on the compactor.

Practical exercise:
- Jointing an area with sand, sweeping, compacting with a guard and re-jointing.
- Test 3: a short written or oral test on the material of module 3.

Learning outcomes of the day:
- The trainee can joint and re-joint an area.
- They know why blocks are compacted only with a guard.
- They know the correct sequence of finishing work and pass Test 3.

Key terms (in Polish):
- Fugowanie (jointing) - filling the joints with sand.
- Zamulanie spoin (filling the joints) - introducing sand into the gaps between blocks.
- Piasek kwarcowy (quartz sand) - clean, fine sand for joints.
- Spoiny stabilizowane (stabilised joints) - joints hardened with a special material.
- Dofugowanie (re-jointing) - topping up the joints after compaction.
- Mata gumowa (rubber mat) - a guard on the compactor protecting the blocks.
- Zageszczenie kostki (compaction of the blocks) - tamping the surface for stability.
- Pielegnacja (aftercare) - caring for the cleanliness and condition of the finished surface.

# MODULE 4 (Week 4): Finishing, quality, supplementary work and employment

Module objective: to round off the trainee's competences and prepare them for work. The participant learns to control the quality of the surface, learns about other general construction work, learns work organisation and work culture, performs an independent final assignment, and finally takes the examination and learns the employment path and the formalities of legal work in Poland. The module ends with the final examination (day 20).

## Day 16: Quality control of the surface

Approximate time: about 7 h (theory about 2.5 h, observation and exercise about 3.5 h, review about 0.5 h, excluding breaks).

Learning objectives of the day:
- Learning the quality criteria for a finished surface.
- Mastering the measurement of flatness and falls.
- Recognising typical defects and ways to repair them.

Theory content:
- Flatness: measurement with a 2-4 m straightedge, the permissible gap (small clearance) under the straightedge.
- Falls: checking conformity with the design and the direction of runoff.
- Pattern and joints: uniformity and appearance.
- No "rocking": a block must not move (wobble) under pressure.
- Typical defects: depressions, unevenness, wrong falls, dirt.
- Repair: relaying a block, correcting the bedding, topping up the joints.
- Acceptance of the surface: what it involves and who carries it out.

Scope of practical observation on the construction site:
- Observation of the acceptance or inspection of a section of the surface.
- A demonstration of measuring with a straightedge and detecting rocking blocks.

Practical exercise:
- Measuring flatness with a straightedge and checking the fall on a laid area.
- Pointing out and describing any defects and proposing a repair.

Learning outcomes of the day:
- The trainee knows the quality criteria for a surface.
- They can measure flatness and fall and detect rocking.
- They recognise typical defects and know how to repair them.

Key terms (in Polish):
- Kontrola jakosci (quality control) - checking whether the work meets the requirements.
- Rownosc (flatness) - the absence of unevenness, measured with a straightedge.
- Przeswit (clearance) - the gap between the straightedge and the surface.
- Klawiszowanie (rocking) - the wobbling of individual blocks.
- Wada (defect) - a fault in the surface (e.g. a depression).
- Zanizenie (depression) - a place lower than it should be.
- Przelozenie kostki (relaying a block) - taking out and laying a block again.
- Odbior (acceptance) - the formal acceptance of the completed work.

# Assessment system, completion requirements, tests and examination

## Components of the assessment

A participant's assessment consists of several components collected throughout the course:

| Component | When | What it checks |
|---|---|---|
| Test 1 (partial) | Day 5 | Organisation of the construction site, OHS, materials, tools (module 1) |
| Test 2 (partial) | Day 10 | Earthworks, sub-base, bordering, bedding (module 2) |
| Test 3 (partial) | Day 15 | Laying blocks, cutting, utility fittings, jointing (module 3) |
| Ongoing practical assessment | Daily | Practical observations and exercises, observation sheets |
| Practical final assignment | Day 19 | Independent completion of a section of surface as a team |
| Final examination | Day 20 | Theory part (test) and practical part (task) |

## Partial tests

The course has three partial tests: after module 1 (day 5), after module 2 (day 10) and after module 3 (day 15). Each test takes the form of a short written or oral assessment, with the possibility of language and translation support for people with weaker Polish. The tests check understanding of the key concepts and rules, with particular emphasis on OHS.

## Ongoing practical assessment

Throughout the course, the trainer keeps observation sheets. In them, they assess how the trainee applies OHS, how they perform the exercises, whether they follow the rules they have learned, how they cooperate in the team and how they care for equipment and material. This assessment carries significant weight, because the course is above all practical.

## Practical final assignment (day 19)

On day 19, the team of trainees independently, under supervision, completes a full section of surface: from the bedding, through laying the blocks and cutting to fit, to jointing and compaction. The work is assessed according to an assessment sheet. Among the things taken into account are: flatness, falls, the quality of the joints and pattern, the quality of the cut pieces, compliance with OHS and cooperation.

## Final examination (day 20)

The final examination has two parts:
- Theory part: a test checking knowledge from the whole course, with particular regard to OHS.
- Practical part: performing a designated paving task on the construction site, assessed according to an assessment sheet.

After the examination, there is a discussion of the results, a conversation about the employment path on the same construction site, and an overview of the formalities of legal work for a foreigner in Poland.

## Course completion requirements

To complete the course and receive a certificate, a participant must meet all of the following requirements together:
- Attendance: presence at classes in accordance with the course requirements.
- Passing the partial tests (days 5, 10, 15).
- A positive ongoing practical assessment (observation sheets).
- A positive assessment of the practical final assignment (day 19).
- Passing the final examination, both theory and practical (day 20).

A graduate who meets all the requirements receives a certificate of completion of the course "Vocational preparation for general construction work (with a specialisation: laying paving blocks)".

## Staff and organisational conditions

The classes are led by a hands-on construction trainer or a paving worker with experience. Participants are provided with language and translation support, access to a working construction site, materials and equipment, personal protective equipment (SOI), and welfare facilities. Safety (OHS) is a priority at every stage of the course.
