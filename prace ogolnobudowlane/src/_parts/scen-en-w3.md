# Lesson-block scenarios - Week 3

## Introduction to Week 3

Week 3 is the heart of the whole course and its specialization. This is the module where the trainee learns the paver's trade (PL: brukarz) in practice: how to lay paving blocks (PL: kostka brukowa) so that the surface is even, durable and good-looking. All the previous weeks (OHS, materials, tools, earthworks, sub-base, bedding) were preparation for this moment. The motto of the week is: "You lay paving from the face, with your head, and along the string line".

In this module the participant goes through the full path of building a paved surface: from the first row as the base, through laying the plane, cutting and fitting, working around manholes and utilities, all the way to jointing and compacting the finished surface. Three things are key: accuracy (even joints and a straight line), repeatability (keeping the pattern) and safety during cutting (silica dust, PL: pyl krzemionkowy).

Each day lasts about 7 hours of classes (plus breaks) and follows a steady rhythm: a theory block in the morning in the welfare container (PL: kontener socjalny) or in a designated area, then observation of real work on an active construction site, then a supervised practical exercise, and finally a short discussion. Day 15 additionally ends with a module recap and partial test number 3.

Abbreviations used in the scenarios:
- PPE - personal protective equipment (PL: SOI - srodki ochrony indywidualnej): helmet, footwear, hi-vis vest, gloves, goggles, hearing protectors, mask.
- OHS - occupational health and safety (PL: BHP - bezpieczenstwo i higiena pracy).
- The trainer leads a multilingual group (PL/EN/ES), supported by an interpreter or trilingual materials.

## Day 11: Principles of laying paving blocks

### Block header

| Item | Content |
|---|---|
| Day | 11 (Monday, Week 3) |
| Module | Module 3: Laying paving blocks (specialization) |
| Topic | Laying patterns, joints 2-5 mm, "from the face" layout, guiding string line, first row as the base |
| Duration | approx. 7 h of classes (plus breaks) |
| Location | Theory block: welfare container / designated area. Practical part: construction site (a plot with finished bedding) |

### Objectives (knowledge, skills, attitudes)

Knowledge - the participant:
- knows the basic laying patterns (running bond / "brick" PL: cegielka, herringbone PL: jodelka at 45 and 90 degrees, stretcher/row layout PL: uklad rzedowy) and understands their different properties (herringbone is the most load-bearing under traffic);
- understands what joints (PL: spoiny) are and why a width of 2-5 mm is maintained (thermal movement, the joint fill, no jamming);
- understands the principle of laying "from the face" (PL: z czola), that is, from the already laid surface, so as not to walk on the prepared bedding;
- knows that the first row sets the line and pattern for the whole field (it is the base).

Skills - the participant can:
- tension the guiding string line and lay the first row of blocks straight along the string;
- keep an even joint between blocks and maintain the chosen pattern;
- work "from the face", without stepping onto the screeded bedding.

Attitudes - the participant:
- understands that the start decides the whole job ("a crooked first row means a crooked whole field");
- works patiently and carefully, checking the joint and line continuously.

### Materials, tools and PPE

Materials: concrete paving blocks (different shapes and colours, thickness 6 and 8 cm), finished bedding on the practice plot, mason's string (PL: sznur murarski), pegs, spacers / dividers for joints (optional), chalk for marking.

Tools: rubber mallet (PL: mlotek gumowy), spirit level (PL: poziomica, libella), aluminium straightedge (PL: lata) 2-3 m, tape measure, builder's square (PL: katownik budowlany), mason's string, pins / pegs for tensioning the string, optionally paver tongs (PL: szczypce do kostki).

PPE: helmet, safety footwear, hi-vis vest, gloves. For kneeling work, knee pads or a kneeling pad.

### OHS issues

- Kneeling work for long periods - knee pads, frequent changes of position, a straight spine when lifting blocks.
- Manual lifting of pallets and blocks - the 8 cm block is heavier; use proper lifting technique, do not overload your back.
- Rubber mallet - strike in a controlled way, keep the fingers of the other hand away from the impact zone.
- Strings tensioned low over the ground are a trip hazard - mark them and take care when crossing.
- Order at the workstation - loose blocks and tools must not lie underfoot.

### Teaching methods

Lecture with pattern drawings on the board, practical demonstration, on-site observation, team exercise, the "show - do with me - do it yourself" method, learning through experience (the participant feels for themselves how the mallet fine-tunes the block).

### Checking and assessment

- Observation sheet: correct tensioning of the string, laying of the first row, keeping the joint, working from the face.
- Result check: straightness of the row (string, straightedge), even joints 2-5 mm, consistency with the chosen pattern.
- Short oral vocabulary check (words of the day).

### Key vocabulary

| Polish term | Short explanation |
|---|---|
| kostka brukowa | concrete surface element (various shapes, thickness 6 or 8 cm) |
| wzor ukladania | the way the blocks are arranged (e.g. running bond, herringbone, row) |
| cegielka | a simple pattern with blocks laid like bricks in a wall |
| jodelka | a diagonal pattern (45 or 90 degrees), very load-bearing under vehicle traffic |
| spoina | the gap between adjacent blocks (kept at 2-5 mm) |
| z czola | laying from the already finished surface, so as not to step on the bedding |
| sznur prowadzacy | a tensioned string that sets the line and direction of laying |
| pierwszy rzad | the initial row of blocks that sets the line and pattern of the whole field |
| mlotek gumowy | a mallet with a rubber head for gently adjusting the block |
| dobicie | lightly setting the block to level and line with the mallet |

### Tips for the trainer (multilingual group)

- Show the patterns visually: lay out a few blocks of each pattern next to each other - an image says more than a name. Link the names "cegielka", "jodelka" to the drawing.
- "From the face" is a hard concept linguistically - show it physically: stand on the laid blocks, not on the bedding, and say "I stand here, not here".
- Show the 2-5 mm joint with a finger and a spacer - a number plus a gesture works across the language barrier.
- Practise short commands: "napnij sznur" (tension the string), "rownaj do sznura" (line up to the string), "dobij kostke" (tap the block down). Repeat in three languages.
- Pair weaker and stronger Polish speakers together for the exercise.

### Common mistakes and how to avoid them

- A crooked first row (ruins the whole field) - lay strictly along the tensioned string.
- Uneven joints (sometimes wide, sometimes narrow) - keep a constant gap of 2-5 mm, check by eye and with a spacer.
- Stepping on the screeded bedding - work only from the face, from the finished surface.
- Mixing patterns or losing direction - stick to one pattern and one guiding line.
- Striking the mallet too hard (the block cracks) - tap gently, feel the block.

## Day 12: Laying the plane

### Block header

| Item | Content |
|---|---|
| Day | 12 (Tuesday, Week 3) |
| Module | Module 3: Laying paving blocks (specialization) |
| Topic | Keeping the pattern and even joints, controlling line and level, mixing blocks from pallets, expansion joints, pace of work |
| Duration | approx. 7 h of classes (plus breaks) |
| Location | Theory block: welfare container. Practical part: construction site (a plot with finished bedding) |

### Objectives (knowledge, skills, attitudes)

Knowledge - the participant:
- understands how to keep a uniform pattern and even joints over a larger surface;
- knows why blocks are mixed from several pallets at the same time (different shades, to avoid colour patches);
- understands what expansion joints (PL: dylatacje) are and when they are needed;
- understands the relationship between pace and work quality (team rhythm).

Skills - the participant can:
- lay a section of the plane keeping the pattern, even joints and the line;
- control the level and evenness of the surface with a straightedge and spirit level, and adjust the blocks with the rubber mallet;
- take blocks from several pallets in order to mix the shades.

Attitudes - the participant:
- cares about the uniformity of the surface (aesthetics), not only the pace;
- cooperates within the team and keeps an even working rhythm.

### Materials, tools and PPE

Materials: paving blocks from several pallets (different shades), finished bedding on the practice plot, mason's string, pegs, chalk.

Tools: rubber mallet, spirit level, aluminium straightedge, tape measure, square, string, optionally paver tongs, a wheelbarrow for feeding blocks.

PPE: helmet, safety footwear, vest, gloves, knee pads. Goggles when any trimming is done.

### OHS issues

- Long work on the knees and bent over - knee pads, change of position, breaks to straighten up.
- Feeding and lifting blocks (especially the 8 cm thickness) - proper lifting technique, alternating work.
- Rubber mallet - controlled strikes, fingers away from the impact zone.
- Order - loose blocks must not lie on the walking route.
- Teamwork - keep safe distances between people, communicate when handing over material.

### Teaching methods

Lecture with a drawing, demonstration, observation of teamwork, exercise in roles, the small-steps method with immediate correction, ongoing feedback.

### Checking and assessment

- Observation sheet: keeping the pattern, evenness of joints, level control, pallet mixing, cooperation.
- Result measurement: surface evenness with a straightedge (allowable gap), even joints, no pattern drift, no colour patches.
- Assessment of teamwork and pace.

### Key vocabulary

| Polish term | Short explanation |
|---|---|
| plaszczyzna | a larger, even surface of laid blocks |
| odcien | a slight difference in block colour between pallets |
| mieszanie palet | taking blocks from several pallets to avoid colour patches |
| plama koloru | a visible area of a different shade (the effect of taking from one pallet) |
| dylatacja | a deliberate break in the surface that allows for material movement |
| rownosc | the absence of surface unevenness, checked with a straightedge |
| poziom | the height of the surface referenced to the planned level |
| korekta mlotkiem | fine-tuning the block to level and line with the rubber mallet |
| tempo pracy | the laying speed that maintains quality (not at the cost of accuracy) |
| rytm zespolowy | the coordinated working pace of the whole crew |

### Tips for the trainer (multilingual group)

- Show pallet mixing through contrast: a field laid from one pallet (a visible patch) next to a field from mixing - the image is more convincing than words.
- Practise "evenness" and "level" physically: let the participant run the straightedge themselves and see the gap under it.
- Introduce pace gradually - first accuracy, then speed. A foreigner may rush out of fear that they are working too slowly; reassure them that quality matters more.
- Practise team commands: "podaj kostke" (hand me a block), "rownaj wzor" (line up the pattern), "sprawdz poziom" (check the level). Repeat in three languages.
- Praise for an even joint motivates more than criticism - praise something specific.

### Common mistakes and how to avoid them

- Pattern drift over a larger field - every few rows check the pattern and line against the string.
- Colour patches (taking from one pallet) - mix blocks from several pallets at the same time.
- Uneven surface level (rocking / "piano-key" effect, PL: klawiszowanie) - control with a straightedge and spirit level, adjust with the mallet.
- Uneven joints - keep a constant gap, check systematically.
- Rushing at the cost of quality - keep the rhythm, but do not give up on checking.

## Day 13: Cutting and fitting

### Block header

| Item | Content |
|---|---|
| Day | 13 (Wednesday, Week 3) |
| Module | Module 3: Laying paving blocks (specialization) |
| Topic | Table saw and angle grinder, splitter (guillotine), cutting at kerbs and manholes, marking out, OHS of cutting |
| Duration | approx. 7 h of classes (plus breaks) |
| Location | Theory block: welfare container. Practical part: construction site (cutting station + laying plot) |

### Objectives (knowledge, skills, attitudes)

Knowledge - the participant:
- knows the tools for cutting blocks (table saw PL: przecinarka stolowa, angle grinder PL: szlifierka katowa, splitter / guillotine PL: gilotyna) and their uses;
- understands how blocks are marked out (PL: trasowanie) for trimming at kerbs, manholes and corners;
- understands the silica dust hazard and the principle of wet cutting.

Skills - the participant can:
- measure and mark the cutting line on a block (marking out);
- safely cut a block with a saw or grinder (blade guard, wet cutting);
- fit the cut pieces at the kerb and insert them while keeping the joint.

Attitudes - the participant:
- treats cutting as a particularly dangerous job - always with full PPE;
- cares about precise fitting of the cut pieces (aesthetics and tightness of the surface).

### Materials, tools and PPE

Materials: blocks for trimming, water for wet cutting, chalk or pencil for marking out, a finished field with a kerb and possibly a practice manhole.

Tools: table saw for blocks (wet), angle grinder with a diamond blade and guard, paving splitter / guillotine (if available), tape measure, square, chalk, rubber mallet.

PPE: helmet, safety footwear, vest, gloves, SAFETY GOGGLES or a face shield, hearing protectors, dust mask (respiratory protection). The full set is mandatory during cutting.

### OHS issues

- Silica dust from cutting blocks is dangerous to the lungs - cut wet (water knocks down the dust), always wearing a dust mask.
- The blade guard must be fitted; the blade must match the material and be checked before work (no cracks).
- Cut away from the body, with the block firmly held, fingers clear of the cutting line.
- Angle grinder: hold with both hands, watch for kickback, do not jam the blade.
- Goggles or a face shield and hearing protectors are mandatory - flying chips and noise.
- The cutting station should be set apart and tidy, electrical cables protected, kept safely away from water (equipment and installation suitable for wet work).

| 2:35-3:15 | Observation of real work on site | Observation of a crew trimming blocks at kerbs and manholes. Participants see how the pavers measure, mark out, cut wet and fit the pieces. The trainer draws attention to PPE and the organization of the cutting station. |
| 3:15-3:45 | Lunch break | - |

### Teaching methods

Lecture with a focus on OHS, demonstration with a discussion of risk, on-site observation, exercise under direct supervision at the machine, workstation instruction, the "safe first, then fast" principle.

### Checking and assessment

- Observation sheet: correct marking out, cutting technique, strict use of PPE, wet cutting.
- Result measurement: fit of the cut pieces (tightness at the kerb), maintained joint, clean cut line.
- OHS assessment - failure to wear full PPE during cutting means removal from the machine and repeated instruction.

### Key vocabulary

| Polish term | Short explanation |
|---|---|
| ciecie | cutting a block to the required size |
| trasowanie | measuring and marking the cutting line on a block |
| przecinarka stolowa | a machine for cutting blocks, often with water cooling |
| szlifierka katowa | a hand-held power tool with a blade, for cutting and grinding |
| gilotyna do kostki | a hand-operated device that splits a block to the required size |
| docinek | a piece of block cut to fill an edge or a corner |
| pyl krzemionkowy | harmful dust produced during cutting, dangerous to the lungs |
| ciecie na mokro | cutting with water, which knocks down the dust and cools the blade |
| oslona tarczy | a guard protecting against flying chips and contact with the blade |
| odrzut (kickback) | a sudden recoil of the grinder when the blade jams |

### Tips for the trainer (multilingual group)

- OHS of cutting is a matter of health and life - practise the key rules ("wet cutting", "mask on your face", "goggles") as simple, repeatable commands in three languages and enforce them strictly.
- Show the dust: cut briefly dry and wet (with due care) - the difference is visible and convincing.
- Practise marking out slowly: measure, mark, cut. Numbers and a mark on the block are understandable beyond language.
- At the machine, always one participant plus the trainer - do not allow cutting without supervision or without full PPE.
- The phrase "stop, zatrzymaj maszyne" (stop, shut the machine down) must be known to everyone in all the group's languages.

### Common mistakes and how to avoid them

- Dry cutting (a cloud of silica dust) - always cut wet and wearing a mask.
- No goggles or hearing protectors - full PPE is mandatory for every cut.
- A badly measured cut piece (too big or too small) - precise marking out, the "measure twice, cut once" rule.
- A crooked cut - guide the blade along the marked line, with the block firmly held.
- Blade jamming and grinder kickback - do not force it, guide it smoothly, hold with both hands.

## Day 14: Falls, drainage and utilities

### Block header

| Item | Content |
|---|---|
| Day | 14 (Thursday, Week 3) |
| Module | Module 3: Laying paving blocks (specialization) |
| Topic | Fitting around manholes, gullies, covers, linear channels, height adjustment, maintaining the fall |
| Duration | approx. 7 h of classes (plus breaks) |
| Location | Theory block: welfare container. Practical part: construction site (area with utilities: manholes, gullies) |

### Objectives (knowledge, skills, attitudes)

Knowledge - the participant:
- understands why the surface must have a fall towards the drainage elements (gullies, channels, manholes);
- knows what a gully (PL: wpust), a cover (PL: wlaz), a manhole (PL: studzienka) and a linear channel (PL: korytko liniowe) are and what role they play;
- understands the principle of fitting blocks around utilities and adjusting the height of the elements to the surface level.

Skills - the participant can:
- set out a fall so that water runs off to the gully or channel;
- fit and trim blocks around a manhole or gully while keeping the joints;
- check whether a utility element is at the correct height relative to the blocks.

Attitudes - the participant:
- cares about the continuity of the fall (water must not stand or flow under a building);
- works precisely around utilities, because these are the spots with the most frequent defects.

### Materials, tools and PPE

Materials: blocks for fitting and trimming, a practice manhole / gully / linear channel (or an element on the active site), water for wet cutting, string, chalk.

Tools: spirit level, straightedge, tape measure, string, rubber mallet, saw and angle grinder (for cut pieces), square, optionally a key for adjusting the cover height.

PPE: helmet, safety footwear, vest, gloves, knee pads. During cutting: goggles, hearing protectors, dust mask.

### OHS issues

- Open manholes and gullies are a risk of falls and injury - secure the openings, do not leave them uncovered.
- Cutting pieces around utilities - full PPE, wet cutting, as on Day 13.
- Kneeling work at the utility elements - knee pads, correct posture.
- Heavy covers and frames - lift using proper technique, with two people if needed.
- Order around the manhole - tools and cut pieces must not block the route or fall into the opening.

### Teaching methods

Lecture with a drawing of falls and a gully cross-section, demonstration, on-site observation, team exercise around a real element, a water test (pouring water to see the run-off direction), feedback.

### Checking and assessment

- Observation sheet: correct setting of the fall, fitting of blocks, height control, OHS during cutting.
- Result measurement: maintained fall to the gully (water test - water runs off, does not stand), tight fit of the cut pieces, element level with the blocks.
- Assessment of the accuracy of the detailing around utilities.

### Key vocabulary

| Polish term | Short explanation |
|---|---|
| spadek | the slope of the surface that lets water run off (e.g. 2%) |
| odwodnienie | carrying water off the surface to gullies and channels |
| wpust | a grate in the surface that collects rainwater |
| wlaz | a cover (with a frame) over a manhole opening, driven and walked over |
| studzienka | an underground sewer element with access through a cover |
| korytko liniowe | a long element collecting water along a line |
| regulacja wysokosci | setting the cover or gully level with the block surface |
| ciaglosc spadku | an uninterrupted, even slope towards the drainage |
| proba wodna | pouring water over the surface to check the run-off direction |
| obrobka | fitting and trimming blocks around an element |

### Tips for the trainer (multilingual group)

- Show the fall and drainage with a water test: pour water and let participants see where it flows - this convinces without words.
- Link the element names (gully, cover, manhole) to the real object - point with a finger and name it.
- Show "element level with the block" with a straightedge laid on the cover and the block - any gap or step is seen at once.
- Practise short commands: "sprawdz spadek" (check the fall), "rownaj do wlazu" (line up to the cover), "dociacie tutaj" (a cut piece here). Repeat in three languages.
- Stress that the areas around utilities are the "showcase" of quality - this is where good or bad work is most visible.

### Common mistakes and how to avoid them

- A broken fall (water stands or flows the wrong way) - control the fall with a spirit level and a water test.
- A utility element too high or too low (step, rocking) - adjust the height to the block level.
- Badly fitted cut pieces around the manhole (gaps) - precise marking out and cutting.
- Uneven joints around a round element - run the joints evenly, adjust with the mallet.
- Cutting pieces without PPE - as on Day 13, full PPE and wet cutting are mandatory.

## Day 15: Jointing and compacting (RECAP + TEST 3)

### Block header

| Item | Content |
|---|---|
| Day | 15 (Friday, Week 3) |
| Module | Module 3: Laying paving blocks (specialization) |
| Topic | Filling the joints (sand, quartz sand), sweeping, compacting with a protective mat, sequence, aftercare; module recap and Test 3 |
| Duration | approx. 7 h of classes (plus breaks) - including the recap and the test |
| Location | Theory block and test: welfare container. Practical part: construction site (a laid field of blocks) |

### Objectives (knowledge, skills, attitudes)

Knowledge - the participant:
- understands what jointing is (filling the joints, PL: zamulanie spoin) and why it is done (stabilization, tightness);
- knows the jointing materials (sand, quartz sand, possibly stabilized joints) and the correct sequence of work (jointing - compacting - re-jointing);
- understands the principle of compacting blocks with a plate compactor (PL: zageszczarka) fitted with a guard / rubber mat (never directly onto the blocks without a guard);
- consolidates the knowledge from the whole of Module 3 (laying principles, the plane, cutting, utilities, jointing).

Skills - the participant can:
- fill the joints with sand and spread the material over the surface;
- compact the laid blocks with a plate compactor fitted with a guard, in the correct sequence;
- re-joint and clean the surface after compacting.

Attitudes - the participant:
- understands that jointing and compacting close out the whole job - without them the surface is not finished;
- approaches the test and the recap with commitment, treating them as a check of their own skills.

### Materials, tools and PPE

Materials: jointing sand (dry), quartz sand, possibly stabilized joints, a laid field of blocks for jointing, a rubber mat / guard for the compactor plate, water, Test 3 sheets (PL/EN/ES), pens.

Tools: broom / brush, plate compactor with a guard (rubber mat), wheelbarrow, shovel, optionally a blower, spirit level and straightedge for the final check.

PPE: helmet, safety footwear, vest, gloves, HEARING PROTECTORS (compactor), goggles and dust mask (dry sand, dust).

### OHS issues

- Plate compactor: hearing protectors are mandatory, hold it firmly, keep feet clear of the plate, guide it steadily.
- A guard (rubber mat) on the plate is mandatory - without it, compacting damages and crushes the blocks (and causes chipping).
- Dry jointing sand creates dust - dust mask and goggles, with light dampening of work if needed.
- Compactor vibration is tiring for the hands - swap the operator, take breaks.
- The test takes place in a safe, quiet area (the container) - without the risks of the site.
- Order at the workstation after the exercise - equipment cleaned, surplus sand collected.

Activating recap (questions, concept map), demonstration, observation, team exercise with individual guidance of the compactor, workstation instruction, a written test as a checking tool, discussion of results as part of learning.

- TEST 3 - a written assessment covering the whole of Module 3 (condition: reaching the pass threshold set in the programme).

### Tips for the trainer (multilingual group)

- Show the "compactor only with a guard" rule through contrast (carefully): a block damaged without the mat vs. an intact one with the mat - the image convinces more strongly than a ban.
- Write the sequence of work (jointing - compacting - re-jointing) as a simple diagram with the numbers 1-2-3 and repeat it like a refrain.
- Prepare the test in three languages (PL/EN/ES) and give more time to people with weaker Polish; consider picture-based questions (point to the correct sequence, mark the element with a guard).
- After the test, always discuss the answers - for a foreigner it is often the discussion, not the score itself, that is the moment of learning.
- Stress that Week 3 closes out the paving specialization - the participant can now lay a surface from the bedding all the way to the joint. This builds motivation before Week 4.

### Common mistakes and how to avoid them

- Compacting without a guard (cracking and chipping of the blocks) - always use the rubber mat on the plate.
- Under-jointed joints (the sand settled, the gaps are empty) - re-joint after compacting, repeat if needed.
- Jointing with wet sand (it does not enter the joint) - use dry sand, with the surface dry.
- Missed spots during compacting (at edges, corners) - systematic, overlapping passes.
- Working without hearing protectors and a mask - mandatory with the compactor and dry sand.
- Leaving surplus sand on the surface (dirt, stains) - sweep and clean once finished.

## Summary of Week 3

After Week 3 the participant has mastered the heart of the paver's trade: they can lay paving blocks from the first row, through the plane and cut pieces, detailing around utilities, all the way to jointing and compacting the finished surface. This is a complete laying cycle on a base prepared with their own hands. Test 3 checks the knowledge of the whole module. Week 4 will move on to quality control, supplementary works, integrated practice and the final examination.

The most important rules to remember from this week:
- The first row is the base - a crooked start means a crooked whole field; lay it along the string.
- Lay from the face, from the finished surface, so as not to step on the bedding; keep the joints at 2-5 mm.
- Mix the blocks from several pallets to avoid colour patches, and check evenness with a straightedge.
- Always cut wet and in full PPE - silica dust is dangerous to the lungs.
- The fall must carry water to the drainage; work most precisely around the utilities.
- Jointing and compacting close out the job; the compactor works only with a guard, in the sequence jointing - compacting - re-jointing.
