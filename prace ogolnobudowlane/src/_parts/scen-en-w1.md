# Lesson-block scenarios - Week 1

## Introduction to Week 1

Week 1 is Module 1 of the course "Vocational preparation for general construction work (with specialization: paving block laying)". It covers days 1-5: introduction to the course and to the construction site, two days of occupational health and safety (PL: BHP), and an introduction to construction materials, tools and equipment. The week closes with the first partial test (Day 5).

The course materials are trilingual (Polish, English, Spanish), and the participants are foreigners with no construction experience and varying levels of Polish. For this reason, every scenario emphasizes: plain language, key Polish construction vocabulary, safety (PL: BHP), and learning through observation of a real, active construction site plus supervised practice.

Motto of the week: "Safety first, then the work". Before a participant touches a single paving block, they must understand where the dangerous areas are, how to protect themselves, what the materials and tools are called, and who on the site is responsible for what. This is the foundation on which the next three weeks of the course are built.

## How to read the scenarios

Every day has an identical structure (third-level subsections):

1. Block header (day, module, topic, duration, location).
2. Objectives (knowledge, skills, attitudes).
3. Materials, tools and PPE (personal protective equipment, PL: SOI).
4. Health and safety points for the block.
5. Lesson flow in a minute-by-minute layout (table with time and steps).
6. Teaching methods.
7. Assessment and evaluation.
8. Key vocabulary (Polish terms plus a short explanation).
9. Tips for the trainer (multilingual group).
10. Common mistakes and how to avoid them.

Abbreviations used in the scenarios:
- SOI (PL) - personal protective equipment (PPE): helmet, safety footwear, high-visibility vest, gloves, goggles, hearing protection, respiratory protection.
- BHP (PL) - occupational health and safety.
- BIOZ (PL) - health and safety plan (a document kept on the construction site).

## Standard daily rhythm

Each day lasts about 7 hours of classes (excluding breaks) and follows a fixed rhythm:

| Part of the day | Approximate time | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| Language warm-up and review | approx. 0.3 h | greeting, review of the previous day, words of the day |
| Theory block | approx. 2.2 h | introduction of a new topic in the site welfare container or a dedicated area |
| Trainer demonstration and on-site observation | approx. 1.5 h | demonstration plus watching real work being done on an active construction site |
| Supervised hands-on practice | approx. 2.0 h | participants practice on their own, the trainer corrects |
| Discussion and summary | approx. 0.5 h | discussion of mistakes, vocabulary review, preview of the next day |

Breaks (morning and lunch) are included in the day but fall outside the class time above. Day 5 additionally contains the first partial test (Test 1).

## Day 1: Introduction to the course and the construction site

### Block header

| Item | Content |
|---|---|
| Day | 1 (Monday, Week 1) |
| Module | Module 1: Introduction, health and safety, and construction basics |
| Topic | Site organization, participants in the construction process and their roles, documents, site rules and discipline, basic construction vocabulary |
| Duration | approx. 7 h of classes (plus breaks) |
| Location | Theory block: welfare container / dedicated area. Practical part: walk-through of an active construction site |

### Objectives (knowledge, skills, attitudes)

Knowledge - the participant:
- knows what a construction site (PL: budowa) is as an organized workplace and what zones occur on it (storage zone, work zone, walkways, welfare facilities);
- knows the main roles on the site: site manager (PL: kierownik budowy), foreman (PL: majster, brygadzista), paver (PL: brukarz), helper (PL: pomocnik), machine operator (PL: operator), and knows who gives them instructions;
- knows what documents are in use on the site (information board, BIOZ plan, construction log, site rules) and what they are for.

Skills - the participant can:
- name in Polish the basic zones and work positions on the site;
- point out who to report a problem or doubt to;
- move around the site along the designated walkways.

Attitudes - the participant:
- understands that discipline and punctuality are a condition of safety, not just good manners;
- adopts the attitude of "I ask when I do not know" instead of guessing.

### Materials, tools and PPE

Materials and aids: board or flipchart, markers, a printed site layout with labeled zones, a trilingual site-roles card (PL/EN/ES), site rules, attendance list, participant ID badges, evacuation plan (to show).

Tools: no working tools on this day (introductory day).

PPE for the site walk-through: helmet, safety footwear, high-visibility vest. The trainer hands out PPE and checks the fit before entering the site.

### Health and safety points

- Entry onto the site only in complete PPE (helmet, safety footwear, high-visibility vest).
- Movement exclusively along the designated walkways, away from working machines and danger zones.
- No entry under a raised load or into the crane work area.
- Obligation to follow the instructions of the site manager and supervisory staff (paraphrase of the employee's duty under Article 211 of the Labour Code: compliance with health and safety regulations and rules).
- The employer has a duty to ensure safe working conditions (paraphrase of Article 207 of the Labour Code); the participant has the right to ask about hazards.

### Lesson flow

### Teaching methods

Lecture with a demonstration on the layout, site walk-through (learning on location), group work, the word-object association method, role-play of short dialogues ("who, to whom, about what"). A large share of visual aids because of language barriers.

### Assessment and evaluation

- Observation sheet: whether the participant correctly puts on PPE and moves along the designated walkways.
- Check of the result of the "site map" exercise (whether the zones are named and placed correctly).
- A short oral check of the day's vocabulary (5 words).
- No point-based grade; descriptive evaluation (passed / needs review).

### Key vocabulary

| Polish term | Short explanation |
|---|---|
| budowa | an organized place where a structure is built; an area with its own rules and zones |
| kierownik budowy | the person responsible for the whole site, its safety and compliance with the design |
| majster / brygadzista | the person leading a crew of workers, gives daily instructions |
| brukarz | a worker specializing in laying paving blocks and surfaces |
| pomocnik | a worker supporting the crew (transport, handing over material, simple work) |
| operator | the person operating a machine (e.g. an excavator, a compaction plate) |
| strefa niebezpieczna | an area where there is an increased risk (e.g. under a load, near a machine) |
| ciag komunikacyjny | a designated route along which one may move around the site |
| plan BIOZ | the health and safety plan, a document setting out the safety rules for a given site |
| dziennik budowy | an official document in which the course of the works is recorded |

### Tips for the trainer (multilingual group)

- On the first day, speak slowly and in short sentences; write every key word on the board in large letters and show a real object or place.
- Hand out the trilingual roles card (PL/EN/ES), but during classes use the Polish names: the card serves only to aid understanding.
- Check understanding through action ("show me where you are allowed to walk"), not by asking "do you understand?".
- Pair up participants who speak Polish better and worse, so the stronger one helps the weaker one.
- From the first day, agree on a single "stop" gesture (e.g. an open palm) that halts any activity; it will be useful throughout the course.

### Common mistakes and how to avoid them

- Entering the site without complete PPE: the trainer checks PPE at every entry, with no exceptions.
- Taking "shortcuts" through work zones: enforce the walkways from the start.
- Staying silent instead of asking when something is not understood: reward questions, do not embarrass.
- Confusing roles (who gives instructions): repeat the chain of command using specific people from the site.
- Lateness and disregard for discipline: clearly link punctuality with the safety of the crew.

## Day 2: Health and safety I - basics and personal protection

### Block header

| Item | Content |
|---|---|
| Day | 2 (Tuesday, Week 1) |
| Module | Module 1: Introduction, health and safety, and construction basics |
| Topic | Legal basis of health and safety, the most common hazards on the site, personal protective equipment (PPE, PL: SOI), safety signs, danger zones, order at the workstation |
| Duration | approx. 7 h of classes (plus breaks) |
| Location | Theory block: welfare container. Practical part: active construction site (observation of PPE use and signs) |

### Objectives (knowledge, skills, attitudes)

Knowledge - the participant:
- knows that health and safety stems from legal regulations (Labour Code, Section X) and is a duty of both the employer and the employee;
- knows the most common hazards on the site (a fall, a hit, being crushed, a cut, noise, dust);
- knows the types of PPE and knows what each of them protects against;
- recognizes the basic safety signs (prohibition, mandatory, warning, evacuation).

Skills - the participant can:
- select and correctly put on a complete set of PPE;
- recognize a danger zone and keep order at the workstation;
- read the meaning of typical safety signs.

Attitudes - the participant:
- treats PPE as a habit, not a nuisance;
- understands that order at the workstation is an element of safety.

### Materials, tools and PPE

Materials and aids: a set of PPE for demonstration (helmets, safety footwear, vests, gloves, goggles, hearing protection, anti-dust half-masks), boards with safety signs, a trilingual signs card, board, markers, short photos or videos of typical hazards.

Tools: no working tools (a theory-and-observation day with a PPE exercise).

PPE: a full set for each participant (helmet, safety footwear, high-visibility vest, gloves, goggles, hearing protection, anti-dust half-mask); on this day the participants learn to use them.

### Health and safety points

- Health and safety as a legal duty: the employer ensures safe conditions and PPE (paraphrase of Article 207 and Articles 237(6)-237(9) of the Labour Code), the employee has the duty to comply with the regulations (paraphrase of Article 211 of the Labour Code).
- An employee may not be allowed to work without the required health and safety training (paraphrase of Article 237(3) of the Labour Code).
- The basic act for construction sites: the Regulation of the Minister of Infrastructure of 6 February 2003 on occupational health and safety during the performance of construction works (Journal of Laws 2003 No. 47 item 401).
- General rules: the Regulation of the Minister of Labour and Social Policy of 26 September 1997 on general provisions of occupational health and safety.
- Practice of the day: complete PPE at all times on the construction site, keeping order, knowledge of signs and zones.

### Lesson flow

| Time | Stage | Activities |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:25 | Language warm-up and review | Review of Day 1 (roles, zones) with quick questions. Words of the day: "zagrozenie" (hazard), "ochrona" (protection), "kask" (helmet), "znak bezpieczenstwa" (safety sign), "porzadek" (order). Repeat out loud. |
| 0:25-1:45 | Theory introduction | Why health and safety (accident statistics in construction, kept simple). Legal basis kept simple (duties of the employer and the employee). The most common hazards. Types of PPE and what they protect against. Safety signs: colors and shapes (prohibition, mandatory, warning, evacuation). |
| 1:45-2:00 | Break | - |
| 2:00-2:45 | Trainer presentation / demonstration | The trainer shows step by step how to put on and adjust each item of PPE; demonstrates correct and incorrect use (e.g. a helmet too loose, goggles on the forehead). Discusses when a half-mask and hearing protection are needed. |
| 2:45-3:45 | Observation of real work on the site | Site walk-through: observation of how workers use PPE, where the safety signs hang, how the danger zones are marked, what order (or lack of it) looks like at the workstations. The trainer comments on good and bad examples. |
| 3:45-4:15 | Lunch break | - |
| 4:15-6:15 | Supervised hands-on practice | Station 1: each participant independently assembles and puts on full PPE; the trainer checks. Station 2: matching safety signs to their meaning (cards). Station 3: "find the hazards" in a photo of a workstation and propose how to put it in order. Group rotation. |
| 6:15-7:00 | Discussion and summary | Discussion of the most common mistakes in putting on PPE. Review of signs and vocabulary. A short picture quiz. Preview of Day 3 (Health and safety II, particularly dangerous work, first aid). |

### Teaching methods

Illustrated lecture, hands-on PPE demonstration, observation in the field, the station method (rotation), the "find the hazard" exercise, picture quiz. Strong visual support (boards, photos, real PPE).

### Assessment and evaluation

- Observation sheet: correctness of putting on a complete set of PPE independently.
- Result of the sign-matching exercise (number of correct answers).
- A picture quiz at the end (signs, hazards).
- Descriptive evaluation (passed / needs review); the results feed into the ongoing practical grade.

### Key vocabulary

| Polish term | Short explanation |
|---|---|
| BHP | occupational health and safety; a set of rules protecting health and life |
| SOI | personal protective equipment; gear protecting the worker's body |
| kask ochronny | protects the head against a hit and falling objects |
| obuwie ochronne | shoes with a reinforced toe cap and a puncture-resistant sole |
| kamizelka odblaskowa | a high-visibility vest that makes the worker clearly visible |
| rekawice ochronne | protect the hands against cuts, abrasions, chemicals |
| okulary ochronne | protect the eyes against flying debris and dust |
| ochronniki sluchu | earmuffs or earplugs protecting against noise |
| polmaska przeciwpylowa | a mask protecting the airways against dust |
| znak bezpieczenstwa | a sign informing about a prohibition, a mandatory action, a warning or an evacuation route |
| strefa niebezpieczna | an area of increased risk, often marked and fenced off |

### Tips for the trainer (multilingual group)

- Teach PPE through demonstration and repetition of the movement, not through verbal description: each participant puts it on and corrects it themselves.
- Safety signs are international and graphic; use this: teach "color + shape = meaning".
- Repeat one short safety command, e.g. "Kask na glowe!" (Helmet on your head!) and check the reaction of the whole group.
- With weaker language skills, use pictograms and photos instead of long text.
- Praise correct PPE fitting immediately: build the habit through positive reinforcement.

### Common mistakes and how to avoid them

- A helmet badly adjusted or worn "for show": practice the adjustment and check the fit.
- Goggles or a half-mask slipped down "because they get in the way": select the right size and explain the consequences of going without (dust, flying debris).
- Ignoring signs because "it is obvious anyway": practice reading the signs until it becomes automatic.
- Mess at the workstation treated as a trifle: show how mess leads to trips and cuts.
- Confusing the colors of signs (red prohibition, blue mandatory, yellow warning, green evacuation): reinforce with a board and a quiz.

## Day 3: Health and safety II - particularly dangerous work and first aid

### Block header

| Item | Content |
|---|---|
| Day | 3 (Wednesday, Week 1) |
| Module | Module 1: Introduction, health and safety, and construction basics |
| Topic | Particularly dangerous work, basics of working at height, hazards near excavations, manual handling, electricity and tools, noise and dust, first aid, procedure in case of an accident, evacuation |
| Duration | approx. 7 h of classes (plus breaks) |
| Location | Theory block: welfare container. Practical part: workstation instruction, first-aid kit, evacuation routes and assembly point on the site |

### Objectives (knowledge, skills, attitudes)

Knowledge - the participant:
- knows which work is particularly dangerous (working at height, in excavations, with electricity) and why;
- knows the permissible limits for manual lifting and the rules of correct lifting;
- knows how to act in case of an accident (the chain: secure, assess, call for help) and where the first-aid kit, evacuation routes and assembly point are.

Skills - the participant can:
- lift and carry a load with the correct technique (straight back, bent legs);
- recognize a hazard near an excavation and when working at height;
- start the basic first-aid sequence and call for help (emergency numbers, information for the services).

Attitudes - the participant:
- does not undertake particularly dangerous work without supervision and safeguards;
- responds to an accident instead of passively observing.

### Materials, tools and PPE

Materials and aids: a first-aid kit (for demonstration), first-aid boards, a manikin for practicing compressions (if available), a safety harness for demonstration, a board of permissible lifting limits, the site evacuation plan, board, markers, training loads (sacks, items of various weights).

Tools: a sample power tool to discuss the rules (without using it), an extension cord with a residual-current device (PL: wylacznik ochronny) for demonstration.

PPE: a full set as on Day 2; additionally, for demonstration, a safety harness and a helmet with a chin strap (work at height).

### Health and safety points

- Particularly dangerous work requires special supervision and safeguards (paraphrase of the Regulation of the Minister of Infrastructure of 6 February 2003 on health and safety during the performance of construction works, Journal of Laws 2003 No. 47 item 401).
- Work at height: protection against a fall (railings, harnesses), never alone without belaying.
- Excavations: the hazard of wall collapse and underground utilities; do not enter an unsecured excavation.
- Manual handling: comply with the permissible lifting limits, lift with technique, use a wheelbarrow and a colleague's help.
- Electricity and tools: equipment in good order, cables without damage, a protective switch; limit noise and dust with PPE.
- Procedure in case of an accident and evacuation: knowledge of the evacuation routes, the assembly point, the location of the first-aid kit, the emergency numbers. Health and safety training stems from the Regulation of the Minister of Economy and Labour of 27 July 2004 on training in the field of health and safety (general and workstation instruction).

| 0:25-1:45 | Theory introduction | Particularly dangerous work and why. Basics of working at height (safeguards). Hazards near excavations and underground utilities. ### Teaching methods

Lecture with a board, demonstration of techniques, workstation instruction in the field, skills practice (lifting, first aid), evacuation simulation, the station method. Emphasis on a repeatable, easy-to-remember action scheme.

### Assessment and evaluation

- Observation sheet: correct technique of lifting a load.
- Practical first-aid check (calling for help, the recovery position).
- Assessment of the reaction to the evacuation signal (time, direction, assembly point).
- Descriptive evaluation (passed / needs review); the result counts towards the ongoing practical grade.

### Key vocabulary

| Polish term | Short explanation |
|---|---|
| praca na wysokosci | work above a defined height, requiring protection against a fall |
| wykop | a hollow in the ground; the walls can collapse, utilities may be inside |
| szelki bezpieczenstwa | a harness protecting against a fall when working at height |
| reczne prace transportowe | moving and lifting loads using human muscle power (manual handling) |
| dzwiganie | lifting a load; permissible weight limits apply |
| taczka | a single-wheel cart for manual transport of material (wheelbarrow) |
| pierwsza pomoc | immediate help to a casualty before the services arrive |
| apteczka | a set of supplies for giving first aid |
| droga ewakuacyjna | a designated escape route in case of danger |
| punkt zborny | the assembly point after evacuation, where attendance is checked |
| wylacznik ochronny | a device cutting off the current in case of a fault, protects against electric shock |

### Tips for the trainer (multilingual group)

- First aid and evacuation must be understandable without words: practice the sequence of movements and the signals (e.g. the audible evacuation signal).
- Write the emergency numbers and the basic sentence for calling for help in large print on the board in three languages and rehearse them out loud.
- Demonstrate the lifting technique and ask everyone to repeat it; correct posture physically (with the participant's consent), because a verbal description is not enough.
- Show the assembly point and the evacuation route physically and walk through them with the whole group: memory of the place works better than a map.
- Stress the right and the duty to stop work in case of a serious hazard: this is not "cowardice".

### Common mistakes and how to avoid them

- Lifting a load "onto the back" with a bent back: teach that the legs do the work, the back stays straight, the load stays close to the body.
- Entering an unsecured excavation: an absolute prohibition without securing and the supervisor's consent.
- Working at height without belaying "just for a moment": the rule is no height without protection.
- Panic or inaction at an accident: reinforce a simple step-by-step action scheme.
- Not knowing the location of the first-aid kit and the assembly point: check this verbally and in the field until everyone knows.

## Day 4: Construction materials

### Block header

| Item | Content |
|---|---|
| Day | 4 (Thursday, Week 1) |
| Module | Module 1: Introduction, health and safety, and construction basics |
| Topic | Aggregates, cement, water, concrete and mortars, concrete paving blocks, edging and kerbs, geotextile; storage, palletizing, manual transport and unloading |
| Duration | approx. 7 h of classes (plus breaks) |
| Location | Theory block: welfare container. Practical part: materials store on the site (pallets of blocks, aggregates, markings) |

### Objectives (knowledge, skills, attitudes)

Knowledge - the participant:
- knows the basic materials for surfaces: aggregates (sand, crushed grit, crushed stone, fractions), cement, water, concrete and mortars;
- knows the types of concrete paving blocks (shapes, colors, thicknesses 6 and 8 cm) as well as edging, kerbs and geotextile;
- knows how to store and transport materials safely (pallets, unloading, manual transport).

Skills - the participant can:
- recognize and name the basic materials in Polish;
- read the basic markings on a pallet of blocks (type, thickness, color, quantity);
- safely pick up and set down a block element or a kerb.

Attitudes - the participant:
- takes care of the material (does not waste, does not damage it) and of order at the store;
- understands that thickness and type of material are chosen to suit the intended use (e.g. 8 cm for heavy traffic).

### Materials, tools and PPE

Materials and aids: samples of aggregates (sand, crushed grit, crushed stone in containers), a bag of cement (for demonstration), samples of 6 and 8 cm blocks in various shapes and colors, a piece of edging and a kerb, a piece of geotextile, pallet labels and markings, board, markers, a trilingual materials card.

Tools: a wheelbarrow, gloves for lifting, possibly a pallet truck for demonstration (without independent operation).

PPE: helmet, safety footwear, high-visibility vest, gloves (mandatory when carrying materials), goggles when working with dusty aggregate.

### Health and safety points

- Manual transport of materials: correct lifting technique (review from Day 3), use of a wheelbarrow, working in a pair with heavy elements.
- Pallets of blocks are heavy and unstable: do not climb on them, do not stand under a pallet being lifted.
- Mechanical unloading (forklift, crane): keep away from the machine's work zone and from the load.
- Cement and aggregate dust irritate the skin, eyes and airways: gloves, goggles, and in case of dusting a half-mask.
- Storage: stable stacking, marking, the passage route clear of material (order = safety).

| obrzeze | a narrow concrete element framing a pavement or a lawn (edging) |
| kraweznik | a larger concrete element separating the roadway from the pavement (kerb) |
| geowloknina | a fabric separating layers of soil and aggregate (separation; geotextile) |
| paleta | a wooden base on which the blocks are transported (pallet) |

### Tips for the trainer (multilingual group)

- Teach materials through touch and comparison: the difference between sand, crushed grit and crushed stone is felt by hand.
- Always place a real sample next to the written word: the word-thing association is the most lasting.
- Markings on a pallet are numbers and symbols understandable beyond language: practice reading them - thickness, color, quantity.
- With a weaker group, teach the 5-6 key materials first, the rest only by visual recognition.
- Link the thickness of the block to its use with a simple image: "6 cm - people, 8 cm - cars".

### Common mistakes and how to avoid them

- Confusing aggregates (sand, crushed grit, crushed stone): practice recognition by touch and sight.
- Wrong choice of block thickness for the intended use: reinforce the 6 cm vs 8 cm rule.
- An unsafe grip on a block or a kerb (fingers underneath): teach a firm, flat grip.
- An overloaded or unstable wheelbarrow: do not overload, load evenly, push, do not pull.
- Working with cement and dust without gloves and goggles: enforce PPE at every contact.

### Objectives (knowledge, skills, attitudes)

Knowledge - the participant:
- knows the basic hand tools (rubber mallet, shovel, rake, wheelbarrow, spirit level, mason's line, straightedge, square) and their purpose;
- knows the measuring tools (tape measure, leveling staff, water level, laser level) and the power tools (cut-off saw, angle grinder);
- knows what a plate compactor and a rammer are for and what the rules of their safe operation are.

Skills - the participant can:
- select a tool for a simple task and name it in Polish;
- carry out a basic measurement (length, level) and a simple setting out;
- safely hand over, carry and set down a tool.

Attitudes - the participant:
- takes care of the tools (cleans them, puts them back in place, reports faults);
- treats power tools and the compactor with due caution.

### Materials, tools and PPE

Materials and aids: a set of hand tools for demonstration and practice (rubber mallet, shovel, rake, spirit level, line, straightedge, square, tape measure), a plate compactor and a rammer (for demonstration and observation), a cut-off saw and an angle grinder (to demonstrate the rules, without independent work), Test 1 sheets in three language versions (PL/EN/ES), board, markers.

Tools: as above, with emphasis on hand and measuring tools for the participants to practice with.

PPE: helmet, safety footwear, high-visibility vest, gloves; during compactor observation additionally hearing protection and goggles; during the cutting demonstration goggles, hearing protection and a half-mask (preview of the Week 3 topic).

### Health and safety points

- Hand tools in good order, without a cracked handle; carry them with blades and cutting parts pointing down.
- Power tools (cut-off saw, angle grinder): the right disc for the material, the guard in place, no loose clothing, goggles and hearing protection; cutting blocks wet limits silica dust (more in Week 3).
- Plate compactor and rammer: operated only by a trained person; noise and vibration - hearing protection, safety footwear, keeping feet away from the plate. Basis: the Regulation of the Minister of Economy of 20 September 2001 on health and safety during the operation of machines and other technical devices for earthworks, construction and road works.
- Hand over tools from hand to hand, no throwing; set them down in the designated place.

### Teaching methods

Lecture with a demonstration, practice at tool stations, observation of a machine at work, an activating review (questions and a quiz), a written test with a practical element. A multilingual test with a large number of pictograms, in order to assess knowledge rather than language skills.

### Assessment and evaluation

- Test 1 (written): a point score; the pass threshold is set in the course rules.
- Practical part of the test: recognizing tools and PPE, safely handing over a tool.
- Observation sheet from the tool exercises.
- The result of Test 1 together with the ongoing grades from Week 1 make up the pass of Module 1. A participant scoring below the threshold receives guidance for review.

### Tips for the trainer (multilingual group)

- Teach tools through use: each participant grips the tool and performs a simple movement, not just listens to the name.
- For the test, make sure each participant has their language version and the pictograms: the test is meant to measure knowledge, not Polish.
- Before the test, work through an example of each question type together (multiple choice, matching, picture), so that the test format is clear.
- Show the compactor only from a safe distance and always in hearing protection: it is a loud machine.
- After the test, give feedback individually and supportively: this is the first check, it should motivate, not discourage.

### Common mistakes and how to avoid them

- Throwing tools instead of handing them over from hand to hand: enforce safe handing over from the start.
- Using an angle grinder or a cut-off saw without a guard and PPE: an absolute prohibition; demonstration only, with the rules.
- Approaching a working compactor too closely: keep a safe distance and hearing protection.
- Language stress during the test blocking knowledge: reassure, provide the language version and time, allow pictograms.
- Putting tools down anywhere (risk of tripping, loss): teach the rule "a tool returns to its place".
