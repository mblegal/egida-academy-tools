# Lesson-block scenarios - Week 4

## Introduction to Week 4 (Module 4: Finishing work, quality, supplementary tasks and employment)

Week 4 is the final module of the course. It closes the whole journey: from the first day on the construction site and the basics of occupational health and safety (PL: bezpieczenstwo i higiena pracy, BHP), through materials, tools, earthworks and the sub-base, all the way to the full cycle of paving-block laying from Week 3. Now the trainee learns how to assess and improve the quality of their own work, gets to know other, supplementary general construction tasks, learns work organisation and site work culture, and finally carries out an independent assessment task and sits the final exam. The last day is also a conversation about real employment on the same construction site, plus a review of the formalities of legal work for a foreigner in Poland.

The module covers 5 working days (Day 16-20), each with about 7 hours of classes (excluding breaks). The daily pattern is fixed:
- a theory block in the morning (about 2.5 h),
- observation of real execution on an active construction site,
- a practical exercise under the trainer's supervision (about 3.5 h of observation and exercises in total),
- a short wrap-up at the end (about 0.5 h).

The last two days have a special character: Day 19 is a practical assessment task (independent execution of a section of pavement, graded according to an assessment sheet), and Day 20 is the final theory-and-practice exam, a discussion of the employment path, the formalities of a foreigner's work, and the issuing of course completion certificates.

### Introductory glossary for the whole module (terms used every day)

| Polish term | Short explanation |
|---|---|
| kontrola jakosci (quality control) | checking whether the work meets the requirements (evenness, slopes, pattern, joints) |
| rownosc (evenness) | absence of surface unevenness; measured with a 2-4 m straightedge |
| lata (straightedge) | a long, straight bar for checking surface evenness |
| przeswit (gap/clearance) | the gap under the straightedge that shows unevenness (the permissible value is usually a few mm) |
| klawiszowanie (rocking) | a defect: a single block wobbles under pressure like a key |
| odbior (acceptance/handover) | the formal acceptance of completed work by a superior or the investor |
| wydajnosc / norma (output / standard) | how much work (e.g. m2 of paving) is done per unit of time |
| brygadzista / majster (foreman) | the direct supervisor of the crew on site |
| zaswiadczenie (certificate) | a document confirming completion of the course |
| zezwolenie na prace (work permit) | a document allowing a foreigner to work legally in Poland |

## Day 16: Quality control of the pavement

### Block header

| Item | Information |
|---|---|
| Day | 16 (first day of Week 4) |
| Week / Module | Week 4 / Module 4: Finishing work, quality, supplementary tasks and employment |
| Topic | Quality control of the pavement: evenness (2-4 m straightedge, permissible gap), slopes, pattern and joints, no rocking, typical defects and repairs, surface acceptance |
| Duration | about 7 h of classes (plus breaks) |
| Location | theory block: site cabin / designated area; practical part: a finished paving field on an active construction site |

### Objectives (knowledge, skills, attitudes)

**Knowledge** - after the class the trainee:
- knows which features of the pavement are checked during quality control (evenness, slopes, pattern, joints, block stability),
- understands what the permissible gap under the straightedge is and how it is measured,
- knows the typical pavement defects (depressions, unevenness, wrong slopes, soiling, rocking) and ways of repairing them,
- knows what surface acceptance involves.

**Skills** - the trainee can:
- check the evenness of the pavement with a 2-4 m straightedge and assess the gap,
- check the slope (straightedge, spirit level, water),
- recognise a rocking block and point out the defect,
- re-lay a single block to remove a minor defect.

**Attitudes** - the trainee:
- looks at their own work critically, like a quality inspector,
- understands that it is better to fix a defect immediately than to hide it before acceptance,
- treats quality as a craftsman's calling card and a condition for further employment.

### Materials, tools and PPE

**Materials:**
- a finished paving field from the previous days (with minor defects deliberately left in to be found),
- jointing sand (for any joint corrections),
- water (for the slope test).

**Tools:**
- a 2 m and a 4 m control straightedge (PL: lata kontrolna) (or a single 4 m one),
- a measuring wedge / set of gauge plates for measuring the gap (or a ruler),
- a spirit level (PL: poziomica),
- a rubber mallet (PL: mlotek gumowy) (for re-laying a block),
- a string line (PL: sznur), a tape measure,
- an acceptance sheet / checklist (a form for the trainee).

**PPE (personal protective equipment, PL: srodki ochrony indywidualnej, SOI):**
- a safety helmet,
- safety footwear (with a toe cap),
- a high-visibility vest,
- work gloves,
- knee pads (recommended when bending and re-laying blocks).

### Health and safety topics

- Moving around an active construction site only along designated routes, keeping eye contact with machine operators.
- Re-laying a block: a correct grip, a straight back, fingers away from the spot being tapped with the mallet.
- Bending and kneeling work during inspection: knee pads, straightening the back, breaks.
- Order at the workstation, so that straightedges and measuring tools do not create an obstacle.
- Watch out for the slippery, wet surface after the slope test with water.

| 0:20-1:30 | (2) Theoretical introduction | What quality control checks: evenness (2-4 m straightedge, permissible gap), slopes (drainage direction, min. 1-2%), pattern and evenness of joints, no rocking. Shows the repair: removes the faulty block, corrects the bedding layer, re-lays it, taps it down, re-joints. |
| 2:10-2:25 | Break | - |
| 2:25-3:10 | (4) Observation of real execution on site | Moving to a place where acceptance or inspection of a finished pavement is taking place. Trainees observe how the foreman / site manager checks evenness and slopes and how they report and fix defects. |
| 3:10-3:40 | Lunch break | - |

### Teaching methods

- Demonstration with commentary (showing inspection and repair).
- An exercise with a checklist (acceptance sheet): learning through assessing.
- Observation of a real acceptance on site.
- Work in pairs (one measures, the other records, then they swap).
- Visual support (photos of typical defects), important with a multilingual group.

### Checking and assessment

- Ongoing observational assessment (observation sheet): correctness of straightedge measurement, defect detection, correctness of the repair.
- Assessment of the completed acceptance sheet (whether the trainee detected the main defects).
- Short control questions: "What do we measure evenness with?", "What is rocking?".

### Key vocabulary

| Term | Explanation |
|---|---|
| kontrola jakosci (quality control) | checking whether the pavement meets the requirements |
| lata kontrolna (control straightedge) | a straight 2-4 m bar for testing evenness |
| przeswit (gap) | the gap under the straightedge that shows unevenness |
| zanizenie (depression) | a spot below the required level (water collects there) |
| klawiszowanie (rocking) | the wobbling of a single block under pressure |
| przelozenie kostki (re-laying a block) | removing and re-laying a block to remove a defect |
| odbior (acceptance) | the formal acceptance of completed work |

### Tips for the trainer (multilingual group)

- Show the gap physically: slide a wedge or plate under the straightedge and let the trainees see and touch the gap. The concept becomes concrete.
- Demonstrate the "rocking" defect by hand: press on a wobbly block and let everyone feel the movement.
- Prepare the acceptance sheet in a pictorial form (icons: evenness, slope, joint, cleanliness), independent of language level.
- Praise finding defects in one's own work: it is not shameful, it is a trait of a good craftsman.
- Enforce the Polish names ("lata", "przeswit", "spadek"), because these are what they will hear during acceptance.

### Common mistakes and how to avoid them

| Mistake | Consequence | How to avoid it |
|---|---|---|
| Inspecting "by eye" only | overlooked unevenness and depressions | always check with the straightedge and spirit level, not just by sight |
| Ignoring a rocking block | a loose surface, a complaint | press the blocks down, re-lay wobbly ones immediately |
| Repair without correcting the bedding layer | the defect returns under load | when re-laying, also correct the bedding layer under the block |
| Hiding a defect before acceptance | loss of trust, the need to break it up | report and fix defects on your own |

## Day 17: Other general construction tasks

### Block header

| Item | Information |
|---|---|
| Day | 17 |
| Week / Module | Week 4 / Module 4: Finishing work, quality, supplementary tasks and employment |
| Topic | Other general construction tasks: minor bricklaying (mortar, hollow block/brick), concreting small elements, basics of renders/spatterdash (overview), housekeeping and auxiliary tasks, cooperation with crews |
| Duration | about 7 h of classes (plus breaks) |
| Location | theory block: site cabin; practical part: selected work stations on an active construction site |

### Objectives (knowledge, skills, attitudes)

**Knowledge** - the trainee:
- knows what basic bricklaying involves (joining a hollow block/brick with mortar, the joint, the level),
- understands what concreting small elements is (edgings, point footings, steps), at an overview level,
- knows the concept of render and spatterdash (overview, without doing it),
- understands the role of auxiliary and housekeeping tasks and of cooperation with other crews.

**Skills** - the trainee:
- prepares simple mortar (proportions, consistency) under supervision,
- lays a few hollow blocks/bricks in line and level (basics),
- helps with minor concreting (passing, spreading, levelling),
- keeps order and efficiently serves the crew (passing material and tools).

**Attitudes** - the trainee:
- is versatile and open to various helper tasks,
- understands that a helper who knows many tasks is more valuable to an employer,
- cooperates with other crews without conflicts.

### Materials, tools and PPE

**Materials:**
- bricklaying mortar (PL: zaprawa murarska) (or the components: cement, sand, water),
- a few hollow blocks / bricks for practice,
- a small amount of concrete or components for minor concreting,
- water.

**Tools:**
- a trowel (PL: kielnia), a float (PL: paca), a mixing paddle / small concrete mixer or a mixing container,
- a spirit level, a mason's string line, a brick hammer,
- a wheelbarrow, a shovel, a bucket,
- a straightedge for levelling the concrete.

**PPE:**
- a helmet, safety footwear, a high-visibility vest,
- gloves (mortar and concrete irritate the skin),
- safety goggles (protection against mortar / concrete splashes),
- knee pads for low-level work.

### Health and safety topics

- Mortar and concrete are alkaline: they irritate the skin and eyes. Gloves and goggles are mandatory; avoid prolonged skin contact with wet mortar.
- Lifting hollow blocks and bags of cement: lifting technique, a straight back, sharing the load.
- Working with the concrete mixer / mixing paddle: hands away from moving parts, a stable setup.
- Cement dust: respiratory protection (a half-mask) when pouring; avoid inhaling it.
- Order with many crews: watch out for other teams' movement, materials and cables.

### Course of the class

| Time | Stage | Description of steps |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00-0:20 | (1) Language warm-up and review | Review of Day 16 (quality control). Vocabulary of the day: zaprawa, pustak, cegla, spoina murarska, beton, tynk, obrzutka. Motto of the day: "a helper does everything that is needed". |
| 0:20-1:30 | (2) Theoretical introduction | An overview of general construction tasks: minor bricklaying (mortar, joining a hollow block/brick, level, joint), concreting small elements (edgings, point footings, steps), basics of render and spatterdash (what they are, what they are for). The role of housekeeping and auxiliary tasks. Cooperation with various crews. |
| 1:30-2:10 | (3) Trainer's demonstration | The trainer mixes mortar (proportions, consistency), lays a few hollow blocks in line and level, checking with a spirit level. Shows minor concreting (pouring, spreading, levelling with a straightedge). Briefly shows the application of spatterdash to a wall (overview). |
| 2:10-2:25 | Break | - |
| 2:25-3:10 | (4) Observation of real execution on site | Observation of selected work on site: bricklaying, concreting of a small element, or another crew's plastering work. The trainer points out the helper's role in each task. |
| 3:10-3:40 | Lunch break | - |
| 3:40-6:00 | (5) Supervised practical exercise | Trainees at the workstations: they prepare mortar, lay a few hollow blocks level, help with minor concreting (passing, spreading, levelling). Station rotation. The trainer supervises proportions, level and health and safety. |
| 6:00-6:30 | (6) Error review and summary | Assessment of the laid hollow blocks (level, joint) and the minor concrete (evenness). A discussion of the helper's role in various tasks. Vocabulary review. Preview of Day 18 (work organisation and quality). |

### Teaching methods

- A theoretical overview with numerous examples (various tasks).
- Demonstration with commentary.
- A station-based exercise (workstation rotation).
- Observation of various crews (getting to know the context of the helper's work).

### Checking and assessment

- Observation sheet: mortar preparation (consistency), laying a hollow block level, helping with concreting, cooperation.
- Assessment of the result (evenness of the laid hollow blocks, evenness of the minor concrete).
- Control questions: "What do we make mortar from?", "What do we check the level with?".

### Key vocabulary

| Term | Explanation |
|---|---|
| zaprawa (mortar) | a mix of cement/lime, sand and water for joining elements |
| pustak (hollow block) | a larger wall element (e.g. of concrete, ceramic) |
| cegla (brick) | a small, rectangular bricklaying element |
| spoina murarska (mortar joint) | the layer of mortar between elements of the wall |
| betonowanie (concreting) | pouring and forming concrete |
| obrzutka (spatterdash) | the first, thin layer under the render that improves adhesion |
| tynk (render/plaster) | the finishing layer on a wall |

### Tips for the trainer (multilingual group)

- This is an overview day: the point is not to master bricklaying, but to show that a helper comes into contact with many tasks.
- Show the difference in mortar consistency physically (too dry, too runny, good): it is easier to understand by touch than by words.
- With mortar and concrete, repeat one hard rule: "gloves always" (these substances irritate the skin).
- The rotating stations let everyone try several tasks despite the language barrier.
- Use photos / pictograms of bricklaying tools from the PL/EN/ES materials.

### Common mistakes and how to avoid them

| Mistake | Consequence | How to avoid it |
|---|---|---|
| Wrong mortar consistency | weak joining, running off | keep the proportions, add water gradually |
| Working with mortar without gloves | irritation, chemical burns of the skin | always wear gloves, avoid prolonged contact |
| Hollow blocks not levelled | a crooked wall | check the level after every element |
| Lack of order with many crews | collisions, accidents, delays | keep the route and materials tidy, give way to other teams |

## Day 18: Work organisation and quality

### Block header

| Item | Information |
|---|---|
| Day | 18 |
| Week / Module | Week 4 / Module 4: Finishing work, quality, supplementary tasks and employment |
| Topic | Work organisation and quality: planning the day, teamwork, pace and output (standards of m2/day), communication with the foreman, reporting problems, care of equipment and materials, work ethics and culture, material economy |
| Duration | about 7 h of classes (plus breaks) |
| Location | theory block: site cabin; practical part: organising a real workstation on an active construction site |

### Objectives (knowledge, skills, attitudes)

**Knowledge** - the trainee:
- knows how to plan the working day and prepare the workstation,
- understands the concept of output and sample standards (e.g. how many m2 of paving per day),
- knows how to communicate with the foreman and report problems,
- understands the principles of ethics, work culture and material economy.

**Skills** - the trainee:
- organises their own workstation (layout of material, tools, movement routes),
- works in a team with a division of roles,
- reports a problem clearly and at the right moment,
- takes care of equipment (cleaning, putting it back in place) and materials (no waste).

**Attitudes** - the trainee:
- is punctual, reliable and cooperative,
- treats equipment and materials as their own,
- understands that good organisation increases the pace without loss of quality,
- builds a reputation as a worker who can be trusted.

### Materials, tools and PPE

**Materials:**
- materials for organising the practice workstation (pallets of paving blocks, bedding layer, tools),
- examples of day cards / plans (a form),
- possibly a board or flipchart for planning.

**Tools:**
- a full set of paving tools from the previous days (for tidying and maintenance),
- tool-cleaning supplies (a brush, rags),
- a tape measure, a string line (for laying out the workstation).

**PPE:**
- a helmet, safety footwear, a high-visibility vest, gloves,
- other PPE depending on the task being practised.

### Health and safety topics

- Good organisation of the workstation is also safety: clear movement routes, no materials underfoot, tools put away.
- Cleaning and maintenance of tools: disconnecting power tools from the supply, no loose parts.
- Communication as an element of health and safety: reporting hazards and breakdowns to the foreman immediately.
- Planning breaks and task rotation to avoid overload and mistakes from fatigue.

### Teaching methods

- A theoretical overview with examples of standards and situations from the site.
- Demonstration of workstation organisation.
- A team exercise (organisation and division of roles).
- Observation of good practice on an active construction site.
- A short case analysis (what went well, what went badly in the team).

### Checking and assessment

- Observation sheet: workstation organisation, teamwork, communication, order and tool maintenance.
- Assessment of the organisational result (whether the team worked smoothly, without standstills).
- Control questions: "Who do you report a problem to?", "Why do we take care of equipment?".

### Key vocabulary

| Term | Explanation |
|---|---|
| plan dnia (day plan) | the set order of tasks for the working day |
| wydajnosc (output) | the amount of work done in a given time |
| norma (standard) | the accepted, expected output (e.g. m2/day) |
| brygadzista / majster (foreman) | the direct supervisor of the crew |
| zgloszenie (report) | informing a superior about a problem or hazard |
| konserwacja (maintenance) | cleaning and keeping equipment in good condition |
| oszczednosc materialu (material economy) | avoiding waste (of blocks, mortar, sand) |

### Tips for the trainer (multilingual group)

- Present standards as indicative and dependent on conditions, so as not to trigger an unhealthy race at the cost of quality.
- A team exercise reveals who naturally cooperates: use this when forming teams for Day 19.
- Practise communication with the foreman using simple, ready-made Polish phrases (e.g. "brakuje kostki" - blocks are missing, "potrzebuje pomocy" - I need help, "tu jest problem" - there is a problem here).
- Show material economy in numbers (a wasted block is a cost): the language of numbers is universal.
- Work culture: lead by your own example (punctuality, respect, order), not just by words.

### Common mistakes and how to avoid them

| Mistake | Consequence | How to avoid it |
|---|---|---|
| No plan / a chaotic workstation | standstills, trips, slower work | plan the order, lay out material and tools in advance |
| Working alone instead of as a team | slower, poorer quality | divide roles, cooperate, pass material to one another |
| Not reporting problems | a minor problem grows into a big one | report to the foreman immediately, clearly and specifically |
| Neglected tools | breakdowns, poorer work, costs | clean and put tools away after work |
| Wasting material | higher costs, a worse reputation | measure before cutting, use offcuts, do not over-pour |

## Day 19: Integrated practice - the practical assessment task

### Block header

| Item | Information |
|---|---|
| Day | 19 (the day of the practical assessment task) |
| Week / Module | Week 4 / Module 4: Finishing work, quality, supplementary tasks and employment |
| Topic | Integrated practice: independent execution of a section of pavement (from the bedding layer, through laying and cuts, to jointing) in a team, under supervision - an assessment task graded according to an assessment sheet |
| Duration | about 7 h of classes (plus breaks) |
| Location | theory block: a short briefing in the cabin; practical part: an assessment workstation on an active construction site |

### Objectives (knowledge, skills, attitudes)

**Knowledge** - the trainee:
- knows the full sequence of pavement execution (bedding layer, laying, cuts, slope, jointing, compacting),
- knows the criteria by which they will be assessed (the assessment sheet),
- understands the quality requirements (evenness, slopes, pattern, joints, cleanliness).

**Skills** - the trainee:
- independently (in a team) executes a section of pavement from the bedding layer to jointing,
- applies the health and safety rules of the whole course without being reminded,
- monitors their own quality on an ongoing basis (straightedge, string line, spirit level).

**Attitudes** - the trainee:
- works independently and responsibly, with limited help from the trainer,
- cooperates in the team under the pressure of the task,
- treats the task like a real job on the construction site.

### Materials, tools and PPE

**Materials:**
- paving blocks (a few pallets, for mixing shades),
- aggregate / mix for the bedding layer,
- jointing sand,
- kerbs / edgings as a baseline (set earlier),
- water.

**Tools:**
- a full set of paving tools: string line, rubber mallet, straightedge, spirit level, screeding rails for the bedding layer, tape measure, set square,
- a cut-off saw / angle grinder / guillotine (PL: gilotyna) for the cuts,
- brooms and a plate compactor (PL: zageszczarka plytowa) with a rubber mat,
- an assessment sheet for the trainer (a form).

**PPE:**
- the full set: helmet, safety footwear, high-visibility vest, gloves, goggles,
- when cutting: hearing protection, a dust half-mask (wet cutting),
- when compacting: hearing protection, anti-vibration gloves,
- knee pads.

### Health and safety topics

- The task covers all the previously learned hazards: cutting (silica dust, wet cutting, full PPE, blade guard), compacting (noise, vibration, rubber mat), lifting, kneeling work.
- The trainer gives a short health and safety briefing before the start and supervises compliance with the rules throughout.
- Safety is one of the assessment criteria: breaking the health and safety rules lowers the score regardless of the quality of execution.
- The assessment workstation is fenced off and marked, away from machine traffic.

| 1:45-2:15 | (4) Observation / workstation preparation | The teams look at their workstations, plan the work, divide roles, prepare material and tools. The trainer checks readiness and PPE. |
| 2:15-2:45 | Lunch break | - |
| 2:45-5:45 | (5) Supervised practical exercise (assessment task) | The teams independently execute the section of pavement: screeding the bedding layer, laying the blocks according to the pattern, cuts at the edge, maintaining the slope, jointing and compacting with the rubber mat. ### Teaching methods

- A practical assessment task (independent execution of the full cycle).
- Teamwork with a division of roles.
- Assessment according to an objective assessment sheet.
- Limited supervision (the trainee works independently, the trainer observes and assesses).
- Feedback after execution (a discussion of the scores).

### Checking and assessment

- **The practical assessment task** is graded according to the assessment sheet: one of the conditions for completing the course.
- Assessment sheet criteria: evenness (straightedge), slope, correctness of the pattern and joints, quality of the cuts, jointing and compacting, compliance with health and safety, independence and cooperation.
- A pass requires meeting the quality minimum and the absence of serious health and safety violations.
- The task result is discussed individually with the trainee (strengths, areas to improve before the exam).

### Key vocabulary

| Term | Explanation |
|---|---|
| zadanie zaliczeniowe (assessment task) | a practical task testing skills, a condition for passing |
| karta oceny (assessment sheet) | a form with criteria by which the trainer grades the work |
| pelny cykl (full cycle) | the whole sequence from the bedding layer to jointing and compacting |
| samodzielnosc (independence) | working without constant help from the trainer |
| kryterium (criterion) | a single element of the assessment (e.g. evenness, slope) |
| samokontrola (self-monitoring) | checking one's own work on an ongoing basis |

### Tips for the trainer (multilingual group)

- Present the assessment sheet criteria pictorially and simply, so that everyone knows what they get points for, regardless of language.
- Form the teams so that skill and language levels complement one another (the stronger supports the weaker).
- During the task, limit your talking: the trainee is to show that they can do it on their own. Intervene only when safety is at risk.
- Note down observations on an ongoing basis, so that the assessment is reliable and can be justified to the trainee.
- Ensure a calm atmosphere: this is a test, but it is not meant to cause stress; the point is to show a real skill.

### Common mistakes and how to avoid them

| Mistake | Consequence | How to avoid it |
|---|---|---|
| Rushing under the pressure of the task | quality errors, a lower score | work calmly, quality counts more than time |
| Skipping self-checking | undetected unevenness and wrong slopes | check the straightedge, string line and slope during the work, not at the end |
| Neglecting health and safety under stress | a lower score, the risk of an accident | follow the health and safety rules automatically, especially when cutting and compacting |
| No division of roles in the team | chaos, standstills | set roles at the start and stick to them |

### Block header

### Objectives (knowledge, skills, attitudes)

**Knowledge** - the trainee:
- is able to revise and demonstrate the knowledge from the whole course (health and safety, materials, tools, earthworks, sub-base, paving-block laying, quality),
- knows the employment path on this construction site (the position of helper/paver, the conditions),
- knows the basic formalities of a foreigner's legal work in Poland (a work permit or a declaration, a contract, an initial health and safety training at the employer, medical examinations).

**Skills** - the trainee:
- solves the theory test,
- carries out the practical task of the exam,
- can introduce themselves and talk about employment (basic phrases).

**Attitudes** - the trainee:
- approaches the exam responsibly and calmly,
- is ready to take up work on a construction site,
- understands the importance of legal employment and of their own documents.

### Materials, tools and PPE

**Materials:**
- theory test sheets (in the trainee's language version, with a pictorial part),

- information materials about employment and the formalities (PL/EN/ES).

**Tools:**
- a set of paving tools for the practical task,
- a cut-off saw / angle grinder / guillotine, a compactor with a rubber mat (depending on the scope of the task),

**PPE:**
- the full set of PPE as for paving work and cutting,
- hearing protection, a half-mask, knee pads depending on the practical part.

### Health and safety topics

- The practical part of the exam covers the same hazards as the work of the course: health and safety is assessed.
- The trainer reminds the trainees of the health and safety rules before the practical part and supervises compliance.
- A review of employment health and safety: the trainee learns that the employer has a duty to carry out an initial health and safety training and to refer them for medical examinations before allowing them to start work.
- A calm, safe organisation of the exam workstation.

### Teaching methods

- A theory exam (a written test with a pictorial part).
- A practical exam (carrying out a task, assessed according to a sheet).
- Individual feedback.
- An informational conversation about employment and the formalities (with PL/EN/ES materials).
- A formal closing (reinforcing the motivation to work).

### Checking and assessment

- **The final theory-and-practice exam** is a key condition for completing the course.
- Theory part: a test on all the material (the pass threshold is set in the course rules).
- Practical part: a task assessed according to the assessment sheet (quality, health and safety, independence).
- The condition for completing the course as a whole: attendance, passing the partial tests (1, 2, 3), positive observation sheets and the practical task (Day 19), a passed final exam.
- The graduate receives a course completion certificate.

### Tips for the trainer (multilingual group)

- The test must have a pictorial version (recognising tools, patterns, defects), so that the result depends on skills, not on language level.
- Provide translation support during the test and during the conversation about the formalities.
- Present the formalities of a foreigner's work as a practical overview (which documents, who arranges them, when), not as a legal lecture, and point out that the employer helps with some of the formalities.
- Treat the closing as a formal occasion: a positive ending reinforces the readiness to take up work.
- Make sure that every graduate knows what their next concrete step is (who to talk to about employment).

### Common mistakes and how to avoid them

| Mistake | Consequence | How to avoid it |
|---|---|---|
| Paralysing stress at the exam | a result below the actual skills | a calm atmosphere, a short review, clear criteria set in advance |
| Neglecting health and safety in the practical part | a lower score, the risk of an accident | apply the health and safety rules automatically, as on a real construction site |
| Lack of orientation in the formalities | difficulties in taking up legal work | note down which documents are needed and who helps to arrange them |
| Confusing the permit with the contract | misunderstandings when taking up employment | remember the difference: legalising work is one thing, the contract is another |

## Summary of Week 4

After this week the trainee completes their full preparation for work on a construction site:
1. can assess and improve the quality of the pavement: evenness, slopes, pattern, joints, rocking (Day 16),
2. knows and has tried other general construction tasks: minor bricklaying, concreting, auxiliary work (Day 17),
3. can organise a workstation and the team's work and take care of equipment and material (Day 18),
4. has independently executed the full pavement cycle as an assessment task (Day 19),
5. has passed the final exam, learned the employment path and the formalities of legal work, and received a certificate (Day 20).

The key habits of the week: critical self-monitoring of quality, the versatility of a helper, good organisation and work culture, independence, and awareness of the formalities of legal employment.

The condition for completing the whole course as a whole: attendance, passing the three partial tests, positive observation sheets and the practical task (Day 19), and a passed final exam (Day 20). The graduate receives a course completion certificate and has a chance of employment as a helper or paver on the same construction site where the course took place.
